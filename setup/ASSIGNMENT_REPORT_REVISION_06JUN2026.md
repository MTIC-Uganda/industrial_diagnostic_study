## 8. Market Analysis — Four Levels Plus 2040 Projections

For every value chain, the market analysis must cover **four geographic levels**:

1. **Uganda (domestic market)** — current size, growth rate, key demand drivers
2. **Regional market (EAC + COMESA)** — current size, Uganda's share, growth trajectory
3. **Continental market (Africa)** — current size, Uganda's realistic access potential
4. **Global market** — current size, Uganda's position and realistic export opportunities

### 2040 Market Projections

Project what the market will be by 2040 at **two levels**:

**For Uganda:** Project market size on the basis of the **Tenfold Growth Strategy**. Do not simply multiply current figures by 10 — use sound logic for each value chain. Ask: if Uganda's economy is ten times larger by 2040, what does that mean for demand in this specific sector? Consider structural transformation, income effects, urbanisation, infrastructure investment, and industrialisation linkages. The goal is to define the market Uganda needs to create and secure ahead of time.

**For all other markets:** Use **business-as-usual growth projections** based on historical growth rates and established forecasts.

## 10. Greening and Sustainability — Per Value Chain

For each value chain, add a dedicated section assessing:

- **Current status of greening and sustainability** in Uganda's version of this value chain
- **Current greening and sustainability solutions** being applied
- **Upcoming or emerging solutions** that would significantly enhance the value chain's competitiveness (e.g. green steel technologies, circular plastics, solar-powered textile mills, etc.)

Frame these not just as environmental requirements but as **competitiveness enablers** — because green production reduces costs, opens export markets, and attracts investment.

## 11. Constraints Analysis — Weighted, Categorised, Data-Backed

For each value chain, the constraints section must:

- **Categorise constraints** (e.g. inputs/raw materials, energy, finance, skills, policy/regulatory, infrastructure, standards/quality)
- **Weight each constraint** to indicate which is most critical to resolving — present this as a weighted ranking, not an unordered list
- **Explain each constraint elaborately** — a constraint that is not clearly understood will not be acted on
- **Use statistics to demonstrate the gravity** of each constraint wherever possible (e.g. Uganda's power tariff vs. regional peers, cost of capital differentials, skills gap percentages)

Cross-cutting constraints (those appearing across multiple value chains) should be noted in the individual chapters and then **fully elaborated in the cross-cutting chapter** of each report.
