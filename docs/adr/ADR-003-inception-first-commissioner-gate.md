# ADR-003: Inception Reports as Mandatory Commissioner Gate

## Status: Accepted

## Context

Before any chapter content was written, the report structure and analytical approach needed to be approved by the MTIC Commissioner. Starting 14 chapters without this approval risked a full structural rework after significant work was complete. Structure changes are cheap at 5 pages; expensive at 50.

## Decision

Two inception reports (one per ToR) were produced as the first formal deliverable. Each inception document stated: the team's understanding of the assignment, the proposed chapter structure with one-paragraph summaries, the data required per chapter, and the timeline. Jerome presented both to the Commissioner before any chapter work began. Chapter execution was gated on Commissioner approval of the inception reports.

## Consequences

Better: caught one structural misalignment (chapter count and framing for Report 2) before execution began; gave the Commissioner visibility and ownership of the approach; created a clear contract for the final deliverable.

Worse: added approximately 3 days before chapters could begin; required a dry-run presentation session with Jerome and Hillary before the Commissioner meeting.

Watch for: inception reports must be specific enough to be binding, not so vague that they allow scope creep at the delivery stage.
