TENFOLD GROWTH FRAMEWORK
🚀
The Tenfold Growth Strategy is the governing purpose of this entire study. All analysis, projections, and prescribed solutions are calibrated to Uganda achieving a tenfold expansion of its economy by 2040 — driven by manufacturing, mineral value addition, and agro-industrialisation (ATMs framework). The question at every step: what gets Uganda to tenfold growth sustainably?

🔗 How The 9 Chains Form One Integrated Industrial System

Minerals → Manufacturing: Iron ore & steel feed Automotive (KMC bus frames ~37,300 t); copper wiring harnesses feed KMC EVs; cobalt feeds EV batteries
Circular flows: Steel slag → cement (SCM); bagasse → grid power; sugar vinasse → bio-fertilizer; plastic waste → cement kilns (alt. fuel)
Petrochemicals unlock Plastics: Kabalega refinery (~2029) → domestic polymer resin → import substitution for USD 37m+ packaging imports
Green advantage: Uganda's hydro grid enables low-carbon steel (scrap-EAF), green ammonia fertiliser, and low-carbon EV buses — a genuine export differentiator

📖 Glossary & Abbreviations
API
Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient — the core chemical compound in a medicine
ATMs
Agriculture
Bagasse
The fibrous residue left after extracting juice from sugarcane — used as a fuel for co-generation
BoU
Bank of Uganda — the central bank; source of trade and private-sector credit statistics
Capacity Utilisation
The share of installed industrial capacity actually being used in production
Downstream
Closer to the final consumer product in a value chain (e.g. rebar fabrication from imported steel)
EAC
East African Community — the regional trade bloc Uganda belongs to
EAF
Electric Arc Furnace — a low-carbon steelmaking process well-suited to Uganda's hydro-powered grid
ERA
Electricity Regulatory Authority — source of electricity consumption/sales data
HS Code
Harmonized System code — the international standard used to classify traded goods (used throughout TradeMap figures)
Import Substitution
Replacing imported goods with domestically manufactured equivalents
KMC
Kiira Motors Corporation — Uganda's state-backed vehicle assembler (buses
Mtpa
Million tonnes per annum — a standard unit for industrial production capacity
NDP IV
Fourth National Development Plan (FY2025/26–FY2029/30) — Uganda's current medium-term development strategy
PCP
Programme for Country Partnership — the UNIDO framework structuring this diagnostic study
PSA
Production Sharing Agreement — the contract type used for the Kilembe copper-cobalt revival
QCIL
Quality Chemical Industries Limited — Uganda's flagship pharmaceutical manufacturer
SCM
Supplementary Cementitious Material — industrial by-products (e.g. steel slag) used to extend cement
Tenfold Growth Strategy
Uganda's ambition to grow the economy roughly tenfold by 2040 through manufacturing and value addition
TradeMap
ITC TradeMap — the International Trade Centre's database of bilateral trade statistics by HS code
UBOS
Uganda Bureau of Statistics — the national statistics office; source of GDP and national accounts data
UNIDO
United Nations Industrial Development Organization
Upstream
Closer to the raw material end of a value chain (e.g. iron ore mining
URA
Uganda Revenue Authority — source of tax-by-sector figures
Value Chain
The full sequence of activities — from raw material through processing to final product — for a given industry
Vinasse
A liquid by-product of sugar/ethanol production — can be processed into bio-fertiliser
WHO-GMP
World Health Organization Good Manufacturing Practice — the quality certification standard for pharmaceutical plants
