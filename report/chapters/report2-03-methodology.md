## 3.1 Overview of the Research Approach

This diagnostic study employed a structured, multi-method research approach designed to produce actionable findings at the value chain level across the six Report 2 chains: Textiles & Garments, Pharmaceuticals, Petrochemicals & Fertilizers, Sugar & Confectionery, Plastics & Packaging, and Cement & Building Materials. The methodology combined desk-based analysis with primary data collection, ensuring that findings reflect both the published evidence base and the on-the-ground realities of Uganda's manufacturing sector.

The study was preceded by an Inception Report that documented the study design, diagnostic framework, data plan, and stakeholder consultation plan. The Inception Report was reviewed and approved by the Technical Working Group (TWG) prior to the diagnostic phase. This chapter summarizes the methodology as executed; the full methodological detail, including diagnostic framework instruments and the data plan, is contained in the Inception Report.

The research was conducted in four sequential phases:

1. **Desk review and secondary data compilation**
2. **Primary data collection — key informant interviews and field visits**
3. **Value chain analysis and prioritization scoring**
4. **Validation and finalization**

---

## 3.2 Desk Review and Secondary Data Compilation

The desk review established the evidence base for each of the six value chains prior to primary data collection. Sources reviewed included:

- **Government policy and planning documents** — NDP IV, NIP 2020, MTIC Strategic Plan, Uganda Vision 2040, Tenfold Growth Strategy, the Cotton Development Organisation (CDO) strategy, the National Drug Policy, the Fertilizer Policy, and sector-specific strategies relevant to the six Report 2 chains.
- **Uganda Bureau of Statistics (UBOS) data** — Annual Survey of Industrial Production, National Accounts data, trade statistics, and the Census of Business Establishments.
- **Uganda Revenue Authority (URA) trade data** — import and export volumes and values by HS code for textiles and garments, pharmaceutical products, chemicals and fertilizers, sugar and confectionery, plastics and packaging, and cement and building materials. This data provided the basis for import substitution analysis and export market sizing.
- **Uganda Investment Authority (UIA) data** — registered investment projects across all six chains, indicative CAPEX, and employment projections.
- **Sector-specific regulatory body data** — National Drug Authority (NDA) for pharmaceuticals; Uganda Sugar Board; Cotton Development Organisation (CDO); Uganda Cement Industry Association.
- **Development partner reports** — World Bank, IFC, African Development Bank, UNIDO, COMESA, and WHO/UNIDO publications on manufacturing sector development in Uganda and East Africa.
- **Regional and global market data** — UN Comtrade, ITC Trade Map, and industry-specific databases (textiles, pharmaceuticals, fertilizers, cement) for market sizing, competitive benchmarking, and export opportunity identification.
- **Private sector and industry association data** — Uganda Manufacturers Association (UMA), Uganda Export Promotion Board (UEPB), pharmaceutical manufacturers' associations, and commodity boards.

Data gaps identified during the desk review were documented and used to structure the primary data collection phase.

## 3.3 Key Informant Interviews

Key informant interviews (KIIs) were conducted with a purposive sample of stakeholders across each of the six value chains. The interview sample covered five categories:

| Category | Examples relevant to Report 2 chains |
|---|---|
| Public sector | MTIC, UIA, URA, UBOS, UNBS, UEPB, National Drug Authority (NDA), Cotton Development Organisation (CDO), Uganda Sugar Board, NEMA |
| Private sector manufacturers | Textile mills, garment manufacturers, pharmaceutical producers, sugar companies, fertilizer blenders, plastics manufacturers, cement plants |
| Industry associations | Uganda Manufacturers Association (UMA), Uganda Pharmaceutical Manufacturers Association, Uganda Sugar Manufacturers Association |
| Financiers and investors | Uganda Development Bank (UDB), commercial banks active in agro-processing and manufacturing, development finance institutions |
| Research and academia | Makerere University (pharmacy, chemistry, engineering), Uganda Industrial Research Institute (UIRI), Kampala International University |

Interviews were conducted using semi-structured guides developed for each value chain. The guides were organized around the six dimensions of the analytical framework (see Section 3.5 below). All interviews were conducted under a standard confidentiality protocol; firm-level data is reported in aggregate or anonymized form throughout this report.

---

## 3.4 Field Visits

Targeted field visits were conducted to representative firms and industrial clusters within each of the six value chains. Field visits served three purposes: verification of desk review findings against observed reality, collection of firm-level production and cost data not available from secondary sources, and assessment of operational constraints that are difficult to capture through interviews alone.

Field visits for the Report 2 chains were conducted at:

- **Textiles & Garments:** Textile mills in Kampala and Jinja; garment manufacturing facilities; cotton ginneries in northern and eastern Uganda.
- **Pharmaceuticals:** Pharmaceutical manufacturing plants in Kampala; National Drug Authority laboratories; Uganda Industrial Research Institute.
- **Petrochemicals & Fertilizers:** Fertilizer blending facilities; petroleum storage and distribution infrastructure; relevant Ministry of Energy facilities.
- **Sugar & Confectionery:** Sugar processing plants (including Kakira Sugar Works, Sugar Corporation of Uganda, and others); confectionery manufacturers in Kampala.
- **Plastics & Packaging:** Plastics manufacturing and recycling facilities in Kampala's industrial areas and Namanve Industrial Park.
- **Cement & Building Materials:** Cement plants; limestone quarries; building materials manufacturers.

## 3.5 Analytical Framework

Each of the six value chains was analysed using a consistent six-part diagnostic framework, applied uniformly to ensure comparability of findings and recommendations across Report 2.

**a. Value Chain Mapping**
The full value chain tree was mapped from primary inputs through all intermediate processing stages to final end products. This mapping identifies where Uganda currently participates in each chain, where value is lost through export of unprocessed agricultural raw materials or import of processed intermediates, and where import substitution and export opportunities exist.

**b. Current State Assessment**
Production volumes, number of active firms, employment, capacity utilization, export values, and import values were compiled for each chain. Key public and private sector players were identified. This assessment establishes the baseline from which growth trajectories and investment requirements are calculated.

**c. Binding Constraints Analysis**
Constraints were assessed across ten dimensions:

| Dimension | Key questions |
|---|---|
| Inputs and raw materials | Availability, quality, cost, and supply reliability of primary inputs |
| Technology and productivity | Vintage and condition of equipment; productivity gap relative to regional peers |
| Energy | Cost, reliability, and access to electricity and thermal energy |
| Water | Availability and cost for industrial and processing operations |
| Logistics | Transport costs, cold chain availability, port access, customs efficiency |
| Waste and effluent management | Compliance costs, environmental liabilities, circular economy opportunities |
| Finance and investment | Access to long-term capital, interest rates, collateral requirements |
| Skills and workforce | Availability of technical, vocational, and managerial skills |
| Standards and quality | Compliance with domestic (UNBS, NDA) and international standards (ISO, WHO-GMP, Codex) |
| Policy and regulatory environment | Tariff structure, non-tariff barriers, licensing, land access, incentives regime |

**d. Market Assessment**
Markets were assessed at three levels: the domestic Ugandan market, the regional East African and COMESA market, and global export markets. For each level: current market size, growth trajectory, Uganda's current market share, the competitive landscape, and Uganda's realistic market access potential.

**e. Prioritization Scoring**
Based on the value chain map, current state, constraints analysis, and market assessment, each identified product or sub-sector within the chain was scored against the five prioritization criteria (see Section 3.6). The 3–4 highest-scoring products or sub-sectors were identified as Uganda's priorities within each chain.

**f. Priority Action Matrix**
For each value chain, a matrix of priority actions was developed, organized by timeframe: quick wins (0–12 months), policy and institutional reforms (1–3 years), and investment interventions (3–5 years).

## 3.6 Prioritization Framework

A transparent, criteria-based scoring framework was applied consistently across all six Report 2 value chains to identify the 3–4 products Uganda should prioritize within each chain. The framework reflects the core analytical principle of this study: **successful industrial economies are built around a small number of products done exceptionally well, not a broad catalogue of marginal interventions.**

Products and sub-sectors within each chain were scored against five criteria:

| Criterion | Description | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Accessible market size | Size and growth rate of the domestic, regional, and export market Uganda can realistically access | 25% |
| 2. Uganda's comparative advantage | Availability of raw material inputs, existing productive base, geographic proximity to key markets | 25% |
| 3. Feasibility | Capital requirements, technology readiness, lead time to commercial scale, management capability | 20% |
| 4. Job creation and income potential | Direct and indirect employment, wage levels, upstream and downstream linkage effects | 15% |
| 5. Import substitution impact | Value of current imports that domestic production could displace; current account benefit | 15% |

Scores were assigned on a 1–5 scale for each criterion and weighted to produce a composite priority score. The scoring and detailed weightings for each chain are documented in the Inception Report. Products scoring in the top quartile across all five criteria were designated priority products.

The framework also requires explicit deprioritization: for each chain, the study identifies which products or sub-sectors Uganda should **not** pursue at this stage, with rationale. With six value chains in scope, the discipline of focused prioritization is especially important — the risk of diffusing attention and resources across too many sub-sectors is higher, and the potential benefit of concentration is correspondingly greater.

## 3.7 Validation

Findings were validated through two mechanisms before finalization:

**Technical Working Group (TWG) review** — draft findings for each value chain were shared with the TWG, comprising MTIC officials, line ministry representatives (Ministry of Health for pharmaceuticals, MAAIF for textiles and sugar, Ministry of Energy for petrochemicals, MoFPED), and development partner technical advisors. TWG feedback was incorporated into the final drafts.

**Stakeholder validation workshop** — a structured workshop was held with TWG members, private sector actors, industry associations, and development partners. The workshop tested the prioritization findings and priority action matrices against participants' knowledge across all six chains and identified any material gaps or contested findings. Workshop outputs are reflected in the final chapter recommendations.

## 3.8 Limitations

This study was conducted under time and resource constraints that imposed the following limitations:

- **Data gaps at the firm level** — Uganda's industrial statistics system does not yet capture comprehensive, up-to-date production, employment, and trade data at the firm level for all six value chains. Where primary data was unavailable, estimates were derived from proxy indicators and triangulated across multiple sources. Data gaps are flagged explicitly in each chapter with `DATA NEEDED` markers where they affect the precision of findings.
- **Breadth of scope** — covering six value chains in one report creates trade-offs in analytical depth per chain compared to a single-chain study. The framework was applied consistently, but chain-specific nuances may be less fully developed than they would be in a dedicated study.
- **Petrochemicals data** — data on Uganda's emerging petrochemical sector is limited given the early stage of the oil and gas value chain. Findings for Chapter 6 are more prospective than empirical.
- **Market data currency** — some secondary market data, particularly for niche sub-sectors, dates from 2022 or earlier. Where data currency is a concern, this is noted in the relevant chapter.
- **Validation sample** — stakeholder consultation was purposive rather than statistically representative. Findings reflect the weight of expert and practitioner judgement rather than a statistically significant survey of the sector population.

These limitations do not undermine the analytical conclusions of the study. They indicate where further data collection and monitoring would strengthen the evidence base for implementation.
