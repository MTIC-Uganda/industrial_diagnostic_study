## 6.A Value Chain Map

The automotive value chain spans inputs (steel, copper, plastics, glass, rubber, electronics), component manufacture, assembly, and after-market parts and service. Uganda is at an **early, deliberately state-anchored stage**: it has a new flagship assembly capability (Kiira Motors) and an active motorcycle-assembly and body-fabrication base, but the market is overwhelmingly served by **imported used vehicles**.

| Phase | Activity | Main inputs | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | Raw materials | Steel, copper, aluminium, plastics, glass, rubber | Material inputs |
| II | Components | Materials | Body panels, frames, seats, wiring, batteries, tyres |
| III | Assembly | Components / CKD-SKD kits | Vehicles, buses, motorcycles |
| IV | Distribution & sales | Vehicles | New & used vehicle sales |
| V | After-market | Spare parts | Parts, service, repair |

**Cross-chain linkage.** This chapter is where Report 1's three chains connect: **steel** (Chapter 4) and **copper/wiring** (Chapter 5) are direct inputs to vehicle manufacture. A domestic automotive anchor pulls demand through the metals chains — the integrated industrial logic behind grouping these three.

**Where value is captured and lost.** The market is met mainly by **imported used vehicles**: Uganda imported **USD 298.4 million of motor cars (HS 8703), USD 169.9 million of goods vehicles (HS 8704), and USD 157.1 million of motorcycles (HS 8711) in 2024**, plus **USD 31.7 million of parts and accessories (HS 8708)** (ITC TradeMap, 2024). Total vehicle and parts imports thus exceed **USD 650 million a year**. By volume, Uganda imports roughly **48,700 used vehicles annually** (Mobility Foresights). Almost all value is captured offshore.

## 6.B Current State Assessment

**Kiira Motors Corporation (KMC) — the anchor.** Uganda's flagship automotive investment reached a milestone on **27 September 2025, when the Kiira Vehicle Plant at Jinja was commissioned** — described at launch as one of Africa's most advanced vehicle plants. Key facts (Uganda Broadcasting Corporation, 2025; Kiira Motors Corporation):
- **Installed capacity ~2,500 vehicles/year**, targeting **10,000 in the medium term**.
- At commissioning, KMC had built **37 electric vehicles and 27 diesel buses**.
- Flagship products include the **Kayoola electric buses**; KMC is positioning for public-transport and institutional fleets.
- KMC reported a financing gap (~UGX 50bn) for FY2025/26 scale-up — capital is the binding constraint on ramp-up.

**Motorcycle and bus-body assembly.** Beyond KMC, Uganda has an established base in:
- **Motorcycle assembly** — firms assemble Indian-brand motorcycles (Bajaj, TVS) from kits for the dominant boda-boda market.
- **Bus and truck body fabrication** — local fabricators build bodies on imported chassis for buses, trucks, and trailers.
- **Spare parts trade** — a large after-market parts sector (e.g. the Ndeeba and Kisekka cluster), currently import-based.

**Trade position (ITC TradeMap, 2024).**

| Segment (HS) | Imports 2024 | Trend | Reading |
|---|---|---|---|
| Motor cars (8703) | USD 298.4m | Rising (USD 281m in 2023) | Dominated by used imports |
| Goods vehicles (8704) | USD 169.9m | High | Trucks/pickups, largely used |
| Motorcycles (8711) | USD 157.1m | Rising | Boda-boda market; CKD assembly opportunity |
| Parts & accessories (8708) | USD 31.7m | Rising | After-market; localisation opportunity |

Exports are negligible — Uganda is overwhelmingly a **vehicle-importing market**, which is precisely the substitution and assembly opportunity KMC and motorcycle assemblers target.

## 6.C Binding Constraints Analysis

| Dimension | Assessment for Automotive |
|---|---|
| **Inputs / raw materials** | Depends on domestic steel/copper (Chapters 4–5, still maturing) and imported components/CKD kits. Local content is currently low. |
| **Technology & productivity** | KMC brings modern assembly technology; component manufacture and tooling remain shallow. EV capability (Kayoola) is a genuine differentiator. |
| **Energy** | Assembly is less energy-intensive than metals; reliable power needed for plant operations; EV strategy aligns with Uganda's hydro-based grid. |
| **Water** | Not a binding constraint. |
| **Logistics** | Landlocked import costs raise CKD-kit and component costs; export of finished vehicles faces the same penalty (~18–20% of sale price). |
| **Waste & effluent** | Paint, solvents, batteries — managed under NEMA; end-of-life vehicle and battery recycling is an emerging issue. |
| **Finance & investment** | The binding constraint for scale-up: KMC's ~UGX 50bn FY25/26 gap; long-term capital for tooling and working capital is scarce. |
| **Skills & workforce** | Automotive/mechatronics and EV skills are scarce; KMC and Makerere/Nakawa VTI partnerships are building them. |
| **Standards & quality** | Vehicle standards, homologation, and used-import age limits shape the market; UNBS and MWT regulation matter. |
| **Policy & regulatory** | Decisive lever: used-vehicle age limits and taxation, public-fleet procurement, and local-content/assembly incentives can pivot demand toward domestic assembly. The 2025 plant gives policy something real to support. |

## 6.D Market Assessment

**Domestic.** Large and growing — over USD 650m/year in vehicle and parts imports, plus ~48,700 used vehicles imported annually. Public-transport electrification (buses) and institutional fleets are an addressable anchor market KMC can serve directly, supported by government procurement.

**Regional (EAC/COMESA).** KMC has signalled regional ambition, including reported interest in **electric-bus exports to West Africa**; the EAC market for buses and commercial vehicles is real, and a "made-in-Uganda" EV bus is a differentiated regional product. Motorcycle assembly can also serve regional demand.

**Global.** Not accessible for finished vehicles in the medium term. The realistic horizon is **domestic substitution (assembly, buses, motorcycles, parts)** plus **regional bus/EV export**.

**Accessible market conclusion.** Prioritize (1) **public-transport and institutional EV/bus assembly** (KMC anchor), (2) **motorcycle CKD assembly** for the mass boda market, and (3) **selective spare-parts localisation**.

## 6.E Prioritized Products

| Product / sub-sector | Market | Comp. adv. | Feasibility | Jobs | Import subst. | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Buses / EV buses (KMC anchor)** | High (public fleets) | High (KMC, hydro grid) | Medium | High | High | **Priority** |
| **Motorcycle assembly (CKD)** | Very high (USD 157m) | Medium | High | Very high | High | **Priority** |
| **Spare parts & components localisation** | High (USD 32m+) | Medium | Medium | High | High | **Priority** |
| Passenger-car assembly (CKD) | High (USD 298m) | Low–Med | Medium | Medium | Medium | Conditional |
| Full domestic car manufacture | High | Low | Low | Medium | Low | Deprioritize |

**The three priorities for Automotive:**

1. **Buses and electric buses (KMC anchor)** — leverage the new plant, the EV differentiator, and government fleet procurement; the clearest state-backed industrial anchor in Report 1.
2. **Motorcycle assembly (CKD)** — the largest-volume mobility market (USD 157m imports); assembly is feasible and job-rich for the boda economy.
3. **Spare-parts and component localisation** — convert part of the USD 32m+ parts import bill and the after-market into domestic manufacture, building forward linkages to steel/copper.

**Explicit deprioritization.** Uganda should **not** pursue full domestic manufacture of passenger cars from the ground up at this stage — global car manufacture is scale- and capital-intensive beyond Uganda's near-term reach. Passenger-car **CKD assembly** is conditional (it can follow once component and supplier ecosystems mature). Focus belongs on **buses/EVs, motorcycles, and parts**, which match Uganda's anchor assets and market.

## 6.F Priority Action Matrix

| Timeframe | Action | Lead | Indicative cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Quick win (0–12m)** | Public-fleet procurement of KMC buses (schools, hospitals, BRT, MDAs) | MoFPED, MoWT, MDAs | Procurement budget |
