## 6.0 Why Automotive Matters for the Tenfold Growth Strategy

Automotive is where Report 1's industrial system pays off: a domestic vehicle industry **pulls demand through steel (Chapter 4) and copper/wiring (Chapter 5)**, converting raw-material processing into high-value manufactured products and skilled jobs. A ten-times-larger economy will move far more people and goods — and today Uganda spends **over USD 650 million a year importing vehicles and parts**, almost all value captured offshore. Capturing even part of that domestically — buses and electric buses, motorcycle assembly, and parts localisation — is both an import-substitution prize and the demand anchor that makes the metals chains viable at scale. Uganda has chosen a **deliberately state-anchored** entry through Kiira Motors, and the **electric-vehicle** angle aligns the chain with Uganda's hydro grid and the green transition.

## 6.A Value Chain Map

| Phase | Activity | Main inputs | Output | Uganda's position |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| I | Raw materials | Steel, copper, aluminium, plastics, glass, rubber | Material inputs | **Maturing** (Ch.4–5) |
| II | Components | Materials | Body panels, frames, seats, wiring, batteries, tyres | **Shallow** |
| III | Assembly | Components / CKD–SKD kits | Vehicles, buses, motorcycles | **Emerging** (KMC; motorcycle assembly) |
| IV | Distribution & sales | Vehicles | New & used vehicle sales | **Active** — used-import dominated |
| V | After-market | Spare parts | Parts, service, repair | **Active** — import-based |

**Cross-chain linkage.** This is where Report 1's three chains connect: **steel** and **copper/wiring** are direct inputs to vehicle manufacture. KMC's bus programme alone is projected to need **~37,300 tonnes of steel for bus frames by 2027/28** (NPA/UDC, 2025) — demand that, if met domestically, pulls the steel chain forward.

**Where value is captured and lost.** The market is met mainly by **imported used vehicles**: in 2024 Uganda imported **USD 298.4m of motor cars (HS 8703), USD 169.9m of goods vehicles (HS 8704), USD 157.1m of motorcycles (HS 8711)**, plus **USD 31.7m of parts and accessories (HS 8708)** (ITC TradeMap, 2024) — over **USD 650 million a year**, with roughly **48,700 used vehicles** imported annually (Mobility Foresights). Almost all value is captured offshore.

## 6.C Market Analysis (four levels) and 2040 Projection

| Level | Size / Uganda position | Drivers |
|---|---|---|
| **Uganda (domestic)** | >USD 650m/yr imports; ~48,700 used vehicles/yr; public-fleet & institutional demand addressable by KMC | Population, urbanisation, public transport, boda economy |
| **Regional (EAC + COMESA)** | KMC signals regional ambition (incl. EV-bus interest); EAC market for buses/commercial vehicles is real | Regional transport demand; a differentiated "made-in-Uganda" EV bus |
| **Continental (Africa)** | Large vehicle-import market; AfCFTA opens assembly/parts access | Africa's young, motorising population |
| **Global** | Not accessible for finished vehicles medium-term; scale-driven | — |

### 2040 projection — Tenfold logic

Motorisation rises steeply with income. A ten-times-larger, urbanising economy implies a **multi-fold increase in vehicle and mobility demand by 2040** — buses for mass transit, commercial vehicles for goods, and motorcycles for the boda economy. The strategic choice is whether that demand is met by **imports (today's USD 650m+ leak, scaling up)** or by **domestic assembly and parts**. The tenfold ambition argues for building assembly capacity (buses/EV, motorcycles) and parts localisation ahead of the demand curve, anchored by public-fleet procurement. Other markets grow on business-as-usual motorisation; Uganda's realistic external play is **regional EV-bus and motorcycle** supply, not global vehicle export.

## 6.D Binding Constraints — Weighted

| # | Constraint (category) | Weight | Evidence / gravity |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Finance / capital for scale-up (finance)** | **5** | The binding constraint: KMC's ~UGX 50bn FY25/26 gap; tooling and working capital scarce and costly |
| 2 | **Shallow local content / components (inputs)** | **4** | Depends on imported CKD/SKD kits and components; domestic steel/copper still maturing (Ch.4–5); low local content |
| 3 | **Demand policy / used-import competition (policy)** | **4** | Used-vehicle age limits, taxation and public-fleet procurement are decisive levers; used imports dominate |
| 4 | **Technology & tooling depth (technology)** | **3** | KMC brings modern assembly, but component manufacture and tooling are shallow; EV capability is a genuine differentiator |
| 5 | **Skills (skills)** | **3** | Automotive/mechatronics and EV skills scarce; KMC–Makerere–Nakawa VTI partnerships building them |
| 6 | **Logistics (infrastructure)** | **3** | Landlocked costs raise CKD-kit/component costs and would burden any vehicle export (~18–20% of sale price) |
| 7 | **Standards & homologation (standards)** | **2** | Vehicle standards, homologation and age limits shape the market; UNBS and MoWT regulation matter |
| 8 | **Energy (energy)** | **2** | Assembly is less energy-intensive; EV strategy aligns with the hydro grid (an advantage, not a constraint) |

Demand-side **policy is the decisive lever**: used-vehicle age limits/taxation and public-fleet procurement can pivot demand toward domestic assembly — and the 2025 plant gives policy something real to support.

## 6.E Greening & Sustainability

Uganda's automotive entry is **green by design** — a rare first-mover advantage: KMC's flagship is the **Kayoola electric bus**, and Uganda's hydro grid means EVs run on low-carbon power.

| Dimension | Status / opportunity |
|---|---|
| **Current status** | EV capability already present (Kayoola); end-of-life vehicle and battery handling under NEMA is an emerging issue |
| **Current solutions** | Electric buses on a hydro grid (low-carbon mobility); local assembly cuts transport emissions vs. imported vehicles |
| **Emerging solutions (competitiveness enablers)** | (1) **EV-bus and e-mobility** as a differentiated regional product; (2) **battery value chain** linked to Kilembe cobalt (Ch.5); (3) **e-boda (electric motorcycle) assembly** for the mass market; (4) **vehicle/battery recycling** and remanufactured parts |

**Why it pays:** an EV-led entry sidesteps competing on legacy combustion vehicles, aligns with global decarbonisation of transport, and links the metals–battery–vehicle system into one green industrial story.

## 6.F Prioritized Products — through the Tenfold Lens

| Product / sub-sector | Market | Comp. adv. | Feasibility | Jobs | Import subst. | Green fit | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Buses / EV buses (KMC anchor)** | High (public fleets) | High (KMC, hydro) | Medium | High | High | High (EV) | **Priority** |
| **Motorcycle assembly (CKD/e-boda)** | Very high (USD 157m) | Medium | High | Very high | High | Medium–High | **Priority** |
| **Spare parts & components localisation** | High (USD 32m+) | Medium | Medium | High | High | Medium | **Priority** |
| Passenger-car assembly (CKD) | High (USD 298m) | Low–Med | Medium | Medium | Medium | Medium | Conditional |
| Full domestic car manufacture | High | Low | Low | Medium | Low | — | Deprioritize |

**The three priorities for Automotive:**

1. **Buses and electric buses (KMC anchor)** — leverage the new plant, the EV differentiator and government fleet procurement; the clearest state-backed industrial anchor in Report 1, and a green one.
2. **Motorcycle assembly (CKD, moving to e-boda)** — the largest-volume mobility market (USD 157m imports); assembly is feasible and job-rich for the boda economy.
3. **Spare-parts & component localisation** — builds local content, supplies both new assembly and the large after-market, and deepens linkages to steel, copper and plastics.

**Explicit deprioritization.** Uganda should **not** pursue full ground-up passenger-car manufacture for export now — it needs scale, deep component ecosystems and capital Uganda cannot yet marshal. Focus on **buses/EV, motorcycles and parts** for domestic and regional markets.

## 6.G Project Profiles

**P1 — KMC bus/EV-bus scale-up (strategic anchor + green).**
- *Strategic:* the flagship that pulls steel and copper demand; clear line to tenfold via mass transit and regional EV-bus export.
- *Quick-win element:* plant commissioned (Sep 2025); public-fleet procurement can fill the order book immediately.
- *Green:* electric buses on a hydro grid.
- *Lead:* KMC + MoFPED (fleet procurement). *Cost:* see Ch.8 (incl. closing the ~UGX 50bn gap).

**P2 — Motorcycle / e-boda assembly (quick win).**
- *Strategic:* captures the USD 157m motorcycle import market; very high jobs in the boda economy.
- *Quick-win element:* CKD assembly is feasible now; shortest lead time.
- *Green:* transition toward electric boda-bodas on the hydro grid.

**P3 — Spare-parts & components localisation (strategic + green).**
- *Strategic:* deepens local content and metals/plastics linkages; supplies assembly + after-market.
- *Quick-win element:* start with high-volume, low-complexity parts.
- *Green:* remanufactured/recycled parts.
