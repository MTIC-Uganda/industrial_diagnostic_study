## 5.0 Why Pharmaceuticals Matters for the Tenfold Growth Strategy

Pharmaceuticals is a **high-value, knowledge-intensive industry and a health-security imperative**. A ten-times-larger economy needs a healthy, productive population and cannot remain ~90% dependent on imported medicines — a vulnerability the pandemic made plain. Uganda already has something rare in the region: a **WHO-GMP, globally-prequalified manufacturer (Cipla QCIL)** exporting across Africa. Scaling local production of essential medicines substitutes a large import bill, builds resilience, and creates skilled jobs — exactly the kind of sophisticated manufacturing the tenfold ambition needs, anchored by guaranteed public and donor demand.

## 5.A Value Chain Map

| Phase | Activity | Main inputs | Output | Uganda's position |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| I | API & excipient supply | Chemicals | APIs, excipients | **Gap** — almost all imported |
| II | Formulation / manufacturing | APIs, excipients | Tablets, capsules, syrups, injectables | **Established** (QCIL et al.) |
| III | Packaging | Bulk product | Packaged medicines | **Active** |
| IV | Distribution | Medicines | NMS/JMS/wholesale supply | **Active** |
| V | Dispensing | Medicines | Pharmacies, hospitals, patients | **Active** |

**Where value is captured and lost.** Uganda imports roughly **90% of its medicines** (down from 96% in 2016); the medical/pharmaceutical import bill was about **USD 534 million in 2023**, with medicaments (HS 3004) imports of **USD 263.4 million in 2024** (ITC TradeMap). The domestic market is ~USD 221m (2023). Local manufacturing adds value at formulation/packaging but depends entirely on imported APIs — the deepest structural gap.

## 5.B Current Status

### Status snapshot

| Indicator | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Domestic market | ~**USD 221m (2023)**, growing ~9.4%/yr | Statista; sector |
| Import dependence | **~90% of medicines** (was 96% in 2016) | UNIDO; sector |
| Medicaments imports (HS 3004) | **USD 263.4m (2024)** | ITC TradeMap |
| WHO-GMP-aligned manufacturers | **6** | NDA; UNIDO |
| Flagship | **Cipla QCIL** — ~1.2bn tablets/capsules/yr; ~UGX 267bn revenue (FY24/25) | QCIL; Cipla |
| Other manufacturers | KPI, Rene Industries, Abacus Pharma | NDA |
| Local pharma employment | +~42% (2017–2019) | UNIDO |

**Local manufacturing base.** Six **WHO-GMP-aligned** manufacturers produce NDA-registered drugs. Flagship **Cipla Quality Chemical Industries Ltd (QCIL)** (est. 2005) has capacity ~**1.2 billion tablets/capsules per year**, producing ARVs, antimalarials and a growing non-communicable-disease (NCD) portfolio, exporting across Africa; FY24/25 revenue ~**UGX 267 billion**. Others: **KPI, Rene Industries, Abacus Pharma**.

**Regulation.** The **NDA** governs registration, importation, manufacturing standards and pharmacovigilance; the **National Drug Register (June 2024)** is the reference.

**Trade position.** Medicaments (HS 3004) imports USD 263.4m dominate (India, Kenya, Europe); QCIL exports regionally but exports are small versus imports.

## 5.C Market Analysis (four levels) and 2040 Projection

| Level | Size / Uganda position | Drivers |
|---|---|---|
| **Uganda (domestic)** | ~USD 221m (2023), +9.4%/yr; ~90% imported — large substitution headroom | Population, disease burden, UHC push, public/donor procurement |
| **Regional (EAC + COMESA)** | QCIL exports ARVs/antimalarials across Africa; donor disease programmes (HIV, malaria, TB) a structured market | EAC medicines harmonisation; donor demand |
| **Continental (Africa)** | Large import-met market; AfCFTA + African Medicines Agency; local-production push | Health security; AMA harmonisation |
| **Global** | WHO-prequalification opens global donor procurement for specific products (QCIL proven) | Donor-funded essential medicines |

### 2040 projection — Tenfold logic

At ~9.4%/yr the market roughly **quadruples to ~USD 0.9–1.2 billion by 2040**, and a ten-times-larger economy with universal-health-coverage ambitions pushes it higher. The strategic goal is to **cut import dependence from ~90% toward a substantial domestic share**, capturing that growth locally in essential generics and consumables, with donor/regional export of prequalified products. Other markets grow on business-as-usual + health-security localisation.

## 5.D Binding Constraints — Weighted

| # | Constraint (category) | Weight | Evidence / gravity |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **API/excipient import dependence (inputs)** | **5** | Near-total dependence on imported APIs — the deepest structural and supply-security risk |
| 2 | **WHO-GMP / quality-systems depth (standards/technology)** | **5** | GMP + NDA registration are the gatekeepers; capability exists (QCIL) but is concentrated; broader industry needs upgrading |
| 3 | **Capital + long regulatory lead times (finance)** | **4** | GMP plants are capital-intensive with multi-year approval cycles; patient capital scarce |
| 4 | **Procurement/market access (policy)** | **4** | Preferential public procurement (NMS/JMS) and NDA fees decisively shape local-production viability |
| 5 | **Skills (skills)** | **3** | Pharmacists, QA/QC and regulatory-affairs specialists in short supply |
| 6 | **Reliable power & pharma-grade utilities (energy/water)** | **3** | GMP needs uninterrupted clean power and pharma-grade water; backup adds cost |
| 7 | **Cold-chain & distribution logistics (infrastructure)** | **3** | Cold-chain and reliable distribution required; landlocked costs raise API prices |
| 8 | **Pharma waste handling (waste)** | **2** | Incineration/effluent regulated under NEMA/NDA |

The two critical constraints — **API dependence** and **GMP depth** — define the strategy: anchor on Uganda's proven GMP strength (QCIL) to scale finished essential medicines now, while selectively building API capability over time.

## 5.E Greening & Sustainability

Green pharma is both a **cost** and a **compliance/market-access** play: GMP buyers and donors increasingly weigh environmental performance.

| Dimension | Status / opportunity |
|---|---|
| **Current status** | Energy- and water-intensive GMP plants; pharmaceutical waste a regulated hazard |
| **Current solutions** | NEMA/NDA waste rules; incineration; QCIL's modern facility standards |
| **Emerging solutions (competitiveness enablers)** | (1) **Solar + reliable power** and energy-efficient HVAC for GMP plants (cuts the high utility cost); (2) **pharma-grade water recycling**; (3) **green chemistry** for any local API/excipient; (4) **proper take-back and incineration** of pharmaceutical waste |

**Why it pays:** lower utility costs improve GMP-product competitiveness, and demonstrable environmental compliance eases WHO-prequalification and donor procurement.

## 5.F Prioritized Products — through the Tenfold Lens

| Product / sub-sector | Market | Comp. adv. | Feasibility | Jobs | Import subst. | Green fit | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Essential generics (ARVs, antimalarials, anti-TB, NCD)** | High | High (QCIL base) | Medium–High | Medium | Very high | Medium | **Priority** |
| **Medical consumables & basic formulations** | High | Medium | High | Medium | High | Medium | **Priority** |
| **Local API / excipient (selective)** | Medium | Low–Med | Low–Med | Medium | High (supply security) | Medium | **Priority (strategic, longer-term)** |
| Biologics / vaccines (fill-finish) | Medium | Low–Med | Low | Medium | Medium | Medium | Conditional |
| Full API synthesis at scale | Low | Low | Low | Medium | High | Low | Deprioritize (for now) |

**The three priorities for Pharmaceuticals:**

1. **Essential generics** (ARVs, antimalarials, anti-TB, priority NCD medicines) — build on QCIL's proven WHO-GMP base and guaranteed domestic + donor/regional demand; the clearest, highest-impact substitution.
2. **Medical consumables and basic formulations** — feasible, job-creating, broad import substitution.
3. **Selective local API/excipient production** — strategic, longer-term, to reduce the deepest dependency where volumes justify.

**Explicit deprioritization.** Uganda should **not** attempt broad-spectrum API synthesis at scale now (capital- and chemistry-intensive beyond current competitiveness); vaccine fill-finish is conditional on regional initiatives. Focus on **finished essential generics** where Uganda already competes.

## 5.G Project Profiles

**P1 — Essential-generics capacity expansion (strategic anchor).**
- *Strategic:* scales QCIL-class WHO-GMP output of ARVs/antimalarials/anti-TB/NCD; cuts the USD 263m import line and serves donor/regional markets.
- *Quick-win element:* preferential NMS/JMS procurement can fill capacity immediately.
- *Green:* solar/efficient GMP utilities.

**P2 — Medical consumables plant (quick win).**
- *Strategic:* substitutes a broad consumables import line; job-rich; lower regulatory barrier than novel drugs.
- *Quick-win element:* shorter lead time than complex formulations.
- *Green:* energy-efficient, low-waste lines.

**P3 — Selective API/excipient facility (strategic, longer-term + green).**
- *Strategic:* attacks the deepest dependency (imported APIs) for high-volume molecules.
- *Quick-win element:* start with one or two high-volume excipients/APIs.
- *Green:* green-chemistry process design.
