## 5.A Value Chain Map

The pharmaceuticals chain runs from active pharmaceutical ingredients (APIs) and excipients through formulation/manufacturing to packaging, distribution, and dispensing. Uganda participates mainly at the **formulation and packaging** stage, importing nearly all APIs, and imports the large majority of finished medicines.

| Phase | Activity | Main inputs | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | API & excipient supply | Chemicals | APIs, excipients (almost all imported) |
| II | Formulation / manufacturing | APIs, excipients | Tablets, capsules, syrups, injectables |
| III | Packaging | Bulk product | Packaged medicines |
| IV | Distribution | Medicines | Wholesale/NMS/JMS supply |
| V | Dispensing | Medicines | Pharmacies, hospitals, patients |

**Where value is captured and lost.** Uganda imports roughly **90% of its medicines** (down from 96% in 2016), with the medical and pharmaceutical import bill at about **USD 534 million in 2023** and medicaments (HS 3004) imports of **USD 263.4 million in 2024** (ITC TradeMap; sector data). The domestic market is around USD 221 million (2023). Local manufacturing adds value at the formulation/packaging stage but depends entirely on imported APIs.

## 5.B Current State Assessment

**Market and import dependence.** Uganda's pharmaceutical market is estimated at ~**USD 221 million (2023)**, growing ~9.4%/year, with **~90% of medicines imported** (Statista; sector analyses). The improvement from 96% (2016) to ~90% reflects deliberate local-production support.

**Local manufacturing base.** Uganda has **six WHO-GMP-aligned manufacturing facilities / local manufacturers** producing NDA-registered drugs (NDA; UNIDO). The flagship is **Cipla Quality Chemical Industries Ltd (QCIL)** — established 2005, with capacity of about **1.2 billion tablets/capsules per year**, producing antiretrovirals (ARVs), antimalarials, and a growing non-communicable-disease portfolio, exporting across African markets; FY24/25 revenue was about Uganda Shilling (UGX) 267 billion (QCIL; Cipla). Other significant manufacturers include **Kampala Pharmaceutical Industries (KPI), Rene Industries, and Abacus Pharma**. Local pharmaceutical employment rose ~42% between 2017 and 2019 (UNIDO sector data).

**Regulation.** The **National Drug Authority (NDA)** regulates registration, importation, manufacturing standards, and pharmacovigilance; the **National Drug Register (June 2024)** is the manufacturer/product reference.

**Trade position (ITC TradeMap, 2024).** Medicaments (HS 3004) imports USD 263.4m — the dominant line — sourced widely (India, Kenya, Europe). Exports exist (QCIL serves regional markets) but are small relative to imports.

## 5.C Binding Constraints Analysis

| Dimension | Assessment |
|---|---|
| **Inputs / raw materials** | Near-total dependence on imported APIs and excipients — the deepest structural constraint and a supply-security risk. |
| **Technology & productivity** | WHO-GMP capability exists (QCIL) but is concentrated; broader industry needs technology and quality-systems upgrading. |
| **Energy** | Reliable power and clean utilities essential for Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP); backup adds cost. |
| **Water** | Pharmaceutical-grade water systems required; a capital and compliance item. |
| **Logistics** | Cold-chain and reliable distribution needed; landlocked import costs raise API costs. |
| **Waste & effluent** | Pharmaceutical waste regulated (NEMA/NDA); incineration and effluent handling required. |
| **Finance & investment** | GMP plants are capital-intensive with long regulatory lead times; patient capital scarce. |
| **Skills & workforce** | Pharmacists, Quality Assurance (QA)/QC, and regulatory-affairs specialists in short supply. |
| **Standards & quality** | WHO-GMP and NDA registration are the gatekeepers; meeting them is the core competitiveness challenge — and Uganda's proven strength via QCIL. |
| **Policy & regulatory** | NDA verification fees, preferential public procurement for local manufacturers, and EAC harmonization shape the local-production trajectory. |

## 5.D Market Assessment

**Domestic.** A ~USD 221m market growing ~9.4%/year, dominated by imports — a large substitution opportunity, anchored by guaranteed public demand (National Medical Stores, Joint Medical Store).

**Regional (EAC/COMESA).** QCIL already exports ARVs and antimalarials across African markets and holds approvals in many countries — proof that Uganda can compete regionally in selected GMP products. Donor-funded disease programmes (HIV, malaria, TB) are a structured regional market.

**Global.** WHO-prequalification opens global donor procurement for specific products (as QCIL has demonstrated); this is the realistic global niche — **prequalified essential medicines**, not broad-spectrum manufacture.

**Accessible market conclusion.** Prioritize **essential generics for domestic + donor/regional markets** (ARVs, antimalarials, anti-TB, key NCD drugs), backed by guaranteed public procurement.

## 5.E Prioritized Products

| Product / sub-sector | Market | Comp. adv. | Feasibility | Jobs | Import subst. | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Essential generics (ARVs, antimalarials, anti-TB, NCD)** | High | High (QCIL base) | Medium–High | Medium | Very high | **Priority** |
| **Medical consumables & basic formulations** | High | Medium | High | Medium | High | **Priority** |
| **Local API / excipient (selective)** | Medium | Low–Med | Low–Med | Medium | High (supply security) | **Priority (strategic, longer-term)** |
| Biologics / vaccines (fill-finish) | Medium | Low–Med | Low | Medium | Medium | Conditional |
| Full API synthesis at scale | Low | Low | Low | Medium | High | Deprioritize (for now) |

**The three priorities for Pharmaceuticals:**

1. **Essential generics** (ARVs, antimalarials, anti-TB, priority NCD medicines) — build on QCIL's proven WHO-GMP base and guaranteed domestic + donor/regional demand. The clearest, highest-impact substitution.
2. **Medical consumables and basic formulations** — feasible, job-creating, and substitutes a broad import line.
3. **Selective local API/excipient production** — a strategic, longer-term move to reduce the deepest dependency (imported APIs), pursued selectively where volumes justify it.

**Explicit deprioritization.** Uganda should **not** attempt broad-spectrum API synthesis at scale now — it is capital- and chemistry-intensive beyond current competitiveness. Vaccine fill-finish is conditional (tied to regional initiatives). Focus on **finished essential generics** where Uganda already competes.
