## 5.A Value Chain Map

The copper value chain runs from ore through refined metal to fabricated products. Uganda's position is unusual and instructive: it has a **historic resource base and an established downstream cable-manufacturing industry, but almost no operating midstream** (mining, smelting, refining) at present.

| Phase | Activity | Main inputs | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | Mining & concentration | Copper ore (+ cobalt) | Copper concentrate |
| II | Smelting | Concentrate | Blister/anode copper |
| III | Refining | Blister copper | Refined cathode (HS 7403) |
| IV | Semi-fabrication | Cathode | Wire rod, bars, profiles (HS 7407/7408) |
| V | Fabrication | Wire rod / cathode | Insulated wire & cable, brass/alloy goods (HS 8544/7409–7419) |
| VI | Market | Finished products | Domestic & regional sales |

**Where value is captured and lost.** The Kilembe mine (Phase I–III) has been **dormant for decades**, so Uganda's cable industry (Phase IV–V) runs on **imported copper rod and cathode**, while the country imports the bulk of its **insulated wire and cable** as finished goods. The trade data quantifies this: Uganda imported **USD 57.7 million of insulated wire and cable (HS 8544) in 2024** — its single largest copper-related import line and rising fast (from USD 48.3m in 2023) — alongside **USD 6.9m of refined copper/cathode (HS 7403)** and **USD 3.1m of copper wire (HS 7408)** (ITC TradeMap, 2024). The midstream gap means almost all upstream value is forgone.

## 5.B Current State Assessment

**Resource base.** The **Kilembe mine** in Kasese District holds estimated deposits **in excess of 6.5 million tonnes of copper ore at 1.77% copper grade, together with about 5.5 million tonnes at 0.17% cobalt grade** (DGSM). This is a significant endowment — cobalt in particular is a high-value battery mineral — but it has not been in commercial production since the 1980s.

**Mine redevelopment (2025).** In a major development, on **3 March 2025 the Government of Uganda signed its first-ever mineral production-sharing agreement**, awarding the redevelopment of Kilembe to **Sarrai Group and Nile Fibreboard Limited**, selected from 14 competing bidders. The plan targets production of **copper cathodes and cobalt metal** (DGSM; Mining Weekly, 2025). This has the potential to re-open the midstream of the chain for the first time in a generation.

**Downstream industry.** Uganda has a real, established copper-using manufacturing base:
- **Cable Corporation Limited** (Mehta Group) — Uganda's **first electrical cable factory (est. 1968)**, supplying cables and conductors to the national utilities (UMEME/UEDCL/UETCL) and holding an estimated **~65% of the domestic market** (Cable Corporation Uganda; Mehta Group).
- **East African Cables** and other regional suppliers serve the balance of the market.

These firms convert imported copper rod and aluminium into building wire, power cables, and conductors — genuine value addition, but dependent on imported metal.

**Trade position (ITC TradeMap, 2024).**

| Segment (HS) | Imports 2024 | Reading |
|---|---|---|
| Insulated wire & cable (8544) | **USD 57.7m** (China, India) | Largest line; finished-goods import dependence |
| Refined copper / cathode (7403) | USD 6.9m (DRC, China) | Imported feedstock for fabrication |
| Copper wire (7408) | USD 3.1m (China, UAE) | Imported semi-fabricate |

Exports across all copper lines are negligible — Uganda is a **net importer at every stage** of the copper chain.

**Global context.** Global refined-copper production was ~27.5 million tonnes in 2024, and demand is structurally rising with electrification and the energy transition; cobalt is a critical battery input (ICSG, *World Copper Factbook 2025*). This strengthens the long-term case for reviving Kilembe — but global cathode markets are scale-driven and Uganda's tonnage is modest.

## 5.C Binding Constraints Analysis

| Dimension | Assessment for Copper & Allied Metals |
|---|---|
| **Inputs / raw materials** | Kilembe ore is dormant; fabricators import all copper rod/cathode. Reviving mining is the central input constraint. |
| **Technology & productivity** | Smelting/refining technology absent domestically; cable fabrication technology is established and competitive. |
| **Energy** | Smelting/refining is energy-intensive; tariff ~5.5 US¢/kWh (ERA 2025) is workable but reliability matters for electro-refining. |
| **Water** | Needed for processing; not the binding constraint. |
| **Logistics** | Landlocked cost penalty (~18–20% of sale price) affects both imported feedstock and any future cathode export. Kilembe–Kasese rail/road links need upgrading. |
| **Waste & effluent** | Historic Kilembe tailings (with cobalt/pyrite) are an environmental liability **and** a potential resource; managed under NEMA. Acid-mine drainage risk requires careful handling. |
| **Finance & investment** | Mine redevelopment is highly capital-intensive and long-lead; the production-sharing model is designed to mobilize private capital. |
| **Skills & workforce** | Mining and metallurgical skills are scarce after decades of dormancy; the cable industry has a skilled base. |
| **Standards & quality** | UNBS standards for electrical cable are critical for safety; substandard cable imports are a market and safety problem. |
| **Policy & regulatory** | The 2025 production-sharing agreement is a major enabling step; mining licensing, tailings rights, and feedstock policy remain to be operationalized. |

## 5.D Market Assessment

**Domestic.** Strong and growing demand for electrical cable, conductors, and building wire driven by electrification, construction, and grid expansion — currently met substantially by imports (USD 57.7m of HS 8544 in 2024).

**Regional (EAC/COMESA).** Cable Corporation already supplies regional utilities; the regional market for cable and conductors is growing with electrification across East Africa. This is Uganda's most accessible export opportunity in the chain — built on existing competitive fabrication.

**Global.** If Kilembe is revived, **copper cathode and cobalt** could in principle reach global markets, but Uganda's tonnage is modest against global scale; cobalt offers a better value-per-tonne export prospect than copper cathode. Realistically, mine output should first **substitute imported feedstock** for the domestic cable industry before targeting global cathode export.

## 5.E Prioritized Products

| Product / sub-sector | Market | Comp. adv. | Feasibility | Jobs | Import subst. | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Insulated wire & cable** | High (USD 57.7m imports) | High (Cable Corp base) | High | High | Very high | **Priority** |
| **Copper cathode & cobalt (Kilembe revival)** | Medium–High | High (reserves) | Medium (long lead) | High | High (feedstock) | **Priority (enabler)** |
| **Copper wire rod / semi-fabricates** | Medium | Medium | Medium | Medium | High | **Priority** |
| Brass / alloy fabricated goods | Low–Med | Low | Medium | Low | Medium | Conditional |
| Primary copper smelting for export | Low (scale) | Low | Low | Medium | Low | Deprioritize |

**The three priorities for Copper & Allied Metals:**

1. **Insulated wire & cable** — expand the existing, competitive Cable Corporation–led industry to capture the USD 57.7m import market and grow regional exports. The fastest, lowest-risk win.
2. **Kilembe copper cathode & cobalt** — execute the 2025 production-sharing redevelopment to revive midstream supply, prioritizing **feedstock for the domestic cable industry** and **cobalt** as the high-value export.
3. **Copper wire rod / semi-fabrication** — the connecting midstream step between cathode and cable, to integrate the chain domestically.

**Explicit deprioritization.** Uganda should **not** target greenfield primary copper smelting for global cathode export as a first move — global cathode is scale-driven and Uganda's tonnage is modest. The value is in **integrating cathode→rod→cable for domestic and regional markets**, and in **cobalt** as the premium export.

## 5.F Priority Action Matrix

| Timeframe | Action | Lead | Indicative cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Quick win (0–12m)** | Enforce UNBS cable standards against substandard imports (safety + market for compliant firms) | UNBS, MTIC, URA | Low |
| Quick win (0–12m) | Finalize Kilembe redevelopment workplan and milestones under the production-sharing agreement | MEMD/DGSM, investor | Low (admin) |
| **Reform (1–3y)** | Develop a cobalt strategy (battery-minerals positioning, off-take, value addition) | MEMD, MTIC | Medium |
| Reform (1–3y) | Tailings management and reprocessing framework at Kilembe (liability + resource) | NEMA, MEMD | Medium |
| Reform (1–3y) | Power and transport upgrades for Kasese/Kilembe | ERA, UNRA, MEMD | Medium–High |
