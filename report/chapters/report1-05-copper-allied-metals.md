## 5.0 Why Copper & Allied Metals Matters for the Tenfold Growth Strategy

Copper is the metal of electrification, and electrification is a precondition for tenfold growth. Uganda has grid access of just **25.3% (UBOS 2024)** — rural access only 9.1% — against a national universal-access ambition; every kilometre of new grid, every new factory and building, and the energy transition itself run on copper wire and cable. A ten-times-larger, electrified economy will need many multiples of today's cable. Uganda is unusual: it holds a **dormant world-class copper–cobalt resource (Kilembe)** *and* an **established, competitive cable industry** that currently runs on imported metal. Joining the two — reviving the mine to feed the mills — is the tenfold opportunity in this chain, with **cobalt** a high-value battery-mineral bonus.

This chapter runs in diagnostic order: status (5.B) → market to 2040 (5.C) → constraints (5.D) → greening (5.E) → priorities and projects (5.F–5.H).

## 5.A Value Chain Map

| Phase | Activity | Main inputs | Output | Uganda's position |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| I | Mining & concentration | Copper ore (+ cobalt) | Copper concentrate | **Dormant** (Kilembe) |
| II | Smelting | Concentrate | Blister/anode copper | **Absent** |
| III | Refining | Blister copper | Refined cathode (HS 7403) | **Absent** — cathode imported |
| IV | Semi-fabrication | Cathode | Wire rod, bars, profiles (HS 7408) | **Weak** — rod imported |
| V | Fabrication | Wire rod / cathode | Insulated wire & cable, alloy goods (HS 8544) | **Strong** — Cable Corporation et al. |
| VI | Market | Finished products | Domestic & regional sales | **Active**, import-supplemented |

**Where value is captured and lost.** Kilembe (Phases I–III) has been **dormant for decades**, so the cable industry (Phases IV–V) runs on **imported copper rod and cathode**, and Uganda still imports most **finished insulated cable**. Trade data quantifies the leak: Uganda imported **USD 57.7 million of insulated wire and cable (HS 8544) in 2024** — its largest copper-related import and rising fast (from USD 48.3m in 2023) — plus **USD 6.9m of refined copper/cathode (HS 7403)** and **USD 3.1m of copper wire (HS 7408)** (ITC TradeMap, 2024). Almost all upstream value is forgone.

## 5.B Current Status

### Status snapshot

| Indicator | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Kilembe reserves | **>6.5 Mt ore @ 1.77% Cu**; ~5.5 Mt @ 0.17% cobalt | DGSM |
| Mine status | Dormant since 1980s; **production-sharing agreement signed 3 Mar 2025** | DGSM; Mining Weekly 2025 |
| Largest import line | Insulated cable (HS 8544) — **USD 57.7m** (2024) | ITC TradeMap |
| Cathode/rod imports (feedstock) | USD 6.9m (7403) + USD 3.1m (7408) | ITC TradeMap |
| Lead fabricator | Cable Corporation Ltd (est. 1968) — **~65% domestic share** | Cable Corporation; Mehta Group |
| Demand driver | Grid access only **25.3%** (UBOS 2024) → large electrification headroom | UBOS |

**Resource base.** The **Kilembe mine** (Kasese District) holds estimated deposits **above 6.5 million tonnes of copper ore at 1.77% Cu**, with about **5.5 million tonnes at 0.17% cobalt** (DGSM) — a significant endowment, cobalt especially being a high-value battery mineral — but not in commercial production since the 1980s.

**Mine redevelopment (2025).** On **3 March 2025 the Government signed its first-ever mineral production-sharing agreement**, awarding Kilembe's redevelopment to **Sarrai Group and Nile Fibreboard Limited** (from 14 bidders), targeting **copper cathode and cobalt metal** (DGSM; Mining Weekly, 2025) — potentially reopening the midstream for the first time in a generation.

### Companies (downstream fabrication)

| Firm | Location | Products | Position |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cable Corporation Ltd** (Mehta Group) | Kampala/Lugazi | Building wire, power cables, conductors | Uganda's first cable factory (1968); **~65% market** |
| **East African Cables (Uganda)** | Kampala | Cables, conductors | Regional supplier; balance of market |
| Other fabricators / importers-assemblers | Kampala area | Wire, cable, alloy goods | Smaller share |

These firms convert **imported** copper rod and aluminium into building wire, power cable and conductors for the utilities (Umeme/UEDCL/UETCL) — genuine value addition, but dependent on imported metal.

### Trade position (ITC TradeMap, 2024)

| Segment (HS) | Imports 2024 | Reading |
|---|---|---|
| Insulated wire & cable (8544) | **USD 57.7m** (China, India) | Largest line; finished-goods dependence (rising from 48.3m in 2023) |
| Refined copper / cathode (7403) | USD 6.9m (DRC, China) | Imported fabrication feedstock |
| Copper wire (7408) | USD 3.1m (China, UAE) | Imported semi-fabricate |

Exports across all copper lines are negligible — Uganda is a **net importer at every stage**.

## 5.C Market Analysis (four levels) and 2040 Projection

| Level | Size / Uganda position | Drivers |
|---|---|---|
| **Uganda (domestic)** | Cable/conductor demand met substantially by imports (USD 57.7m HS 8544, 2024) | Electrification (grid only 25.3%), construction, grid expansion |
| **Regional (EAC + COMESA)** | Cable Corporation already supplies regional utilities; growing electrification across East Africa | Regional grid build-out; EAC market access |
| **Continental (Africa)** | Large, import-met cable deficit; AfCFTA opens access; DRC cathode/cobalt nearby | Africa-wide electrification; battery-minerals demand |
| **Global** | Refined copper ~27.5 Mt/yr; demand structurally rising with the energy transition; cobalt a critical battery input | Scale-driven — Uganda's tonnage modest; cobalt the better value-per-tonne export |

### 2040 projection — Tenfold logic

Uganda's electrified, ten-times-larger economy implies a step-change in cable and conductor demand. With grid access rising from **25.3% toward universal access**, population to ~60m+, and an industrialising, urbanising economy, **domestic cable/conductor demand could plausibly grow several-fold to the order of USD 300–500m+ a year by 2040** (indicative; to refine with UBOS grid-expansion and construction data). Two implications:

1. **A revived Kilembe should first substitute imported feedstock** (cathode/rod) for the domestic cable industry, then export **cobalt** (premium, battery-grade) and surplus cathode regionally/continentally.
2. Other markets (regional, continental, global) grow on business-as-usual energy-transition demand (~3–4%/yr), where Uganda competes in **finished cable regionally** and **cobalt globally**, not commodity cathode.

## 5.D Binding Constraints — Weighted

| # | Constraint (category) | Weight | Evidence / gravity |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Dormant midstream / feedstock (inputs)** | **5** | Kilembe idle since 1980s; fabricators import all cathode/rod; reviving mining is the central constraint |
| 2 | **Capital & long lead time (finance)** | **5** | Mine redevelopment is highly capital-intensive and multi-year; the production-sharing model is designed to mobilise private capital |
| 3 | **Energy reliability (energy)** | **4** | Electro-refining is power-intensive and continuous; reliability (not just tariff) is decisive |
| 4 | **Logistics / Kasese access (infrastructure)** | **3** | Landlocked penalty (~18–20% of sale price); Kilembe–Kasese rail/road need upgrading for ore and cathode |
| 5 | **Tailings & environmental liability (waste)** | **3** | Historic tailings (cobalt/pyrite) are a liability **and** a resource; acid-mine-drainage risk; managed under NEMA |
| 6 | **Standards & substandard imports (standards)** | **3** | UNBS cable standards are safety-critical; substandard imported cable is a market and safety problem |
| 7 | **Skills (skills)** | **3** | Mining/metallurgical skills scarce after decades of dormancy; cable industry has a skilled base |
| 8 | **Policy operationalisation (policy)** | **2** | 2025 PSA is a major enabler; licensing, tailings rights and feedstock policy still to be operationalised |

The two critical constraints — the **dormant midstream** and the **capital/lead-time** to revive it — are exactly what the 2025 production-sharing agreement is meant to solve; execution is now the test.

## 5.E Greening & Sustainability

Copper and cobalt are **enabling minerals for the green transition** — and Uganda's hydro grid makes low-carbon refining feasible, a genuine competitiveness edge.

| Dimension | Status / opportunity |
|---|---|
| **Current status** | No domestic mining/refining footprint to green yet; cable fabrication is relatively low-impact |
| **Current solutions** | Hydro-powered fabrication; copper is infinitely recyclable (scrap-cable recovery is an opportunity) |
| **Emerging solutions (competitiveness enablers)** | (1) **Hydro-powered electro-refining** for low-carbon cathode — marketable into a carbon-conscious world; (2) **tailings reprocessing** to recover cobalt/copper from the historic Kilembe dumps (turns liability into resource); (3) **copper-scrap recycling** to supplement feedstock; (4) **cobalt for batteries/EVs** positions Uganda in the energy-transition value chain (links to the Automotive EV story, Chapter 6) |

**Why it pays:** low-carbon, recycled-content copper and responsibly-sourced cobalt command access and premiums in green-procurement and battery supply chains.

## 5.F Prioritized Products — through the Tenfold Lens

| Product / sub-sector | Market | Comp. adv. | Feasibility | Jobs | Import subst. | Green fit | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Insulated wire & cable** | High (USD 57.7m imports) | High (Cable Corp base) | High | High | Very high | High (hydro) | **Priority** |
| **Copper cathode & cobalt (Kilembe)** | Medium–High | High (reserves) | Medium (long lead) | High | High (feedstock) | High (battery) | **Priority (enabler)** |
| **Copper wire rod / semi-fabricates** | Medium | Medium | Medium | Medium | High | Medium | **Priority** |
| Brass / alloy fabricated goods | Low–Med | Low | Medium | Low | Medium | — | Conditional |
| Primary smelting for global cathode export | Low (scale) | Low | Low | Medium | Low | — | Deprioritize |

**The three priorities for Copper & Allied Metals:**

1. **Insulated wire & cable** — expand the existing, competitive Cable Corporation–led industry to capture the USD 57.7m import market and grow regional exports; the fastest, lowest-risk, electrification-driven win.
2. **Kilembe copper cathode & cobalt** — execute the 2025 production-sharing redevelopment, prioritising **feedstock for the domestic cable industry** and **cobalt** as the high-value, green-transition export.
3. **Copper wire rod / semi-fabrication** — the connecting midstream between cathode and cable, to integrate the chain domestically.

**Explicit deprioritization.** Uganda should **not** target greenfield primary copper smelting for global cathode export as a first move — global cathode is scale-driven and Uganda's tonnage is modest. Value lies in **integrating cathode→rod→cable for domestic and regional markets**, and in **cobalt** as the premium export.

## 5.G Project Profiles

**P1 — Kilembe redevelopment: cathode + cobalt (strategic anchor).**
- *Strategic:* reopens the midstream; feeds the domestic cable industry and positions Uganda in battery-minerals.
- *Quick-win element:* the 2025 PSA is signed — early milestones (workplan, tailings reprocessing) can start now.
- *Green:* hydro-powered refining; tailings recovery; cobalt for the energy transition.
- *Lead:* Sarrai/Nile Fibreboard + MEMD/DGSM. *Cost:* see Ch.8.

**P2 — Cable & conductor capacity expansion (quick win + strategic).**
- *Strategic:* captures the USD 57.7m import market and rising electrification demand; builds regional export.
- *Quick-win element:* expands a proven, competitive industry (Cable Corporation) — shortest lead time.
- *Green:* hydro-powered; scrap-copper recycling.

**P3 — Cobalt/tailings reprocessing (green + quick-ish).**
- *Strategic:* extracts value from historic Kilembe tailings; battery-mineral positioning.
- *Quick-win element:* reprocessing existing dumps avoids full greenfield mine lead time.
- *Green:* remediates an environmental liability while recovering metal.
