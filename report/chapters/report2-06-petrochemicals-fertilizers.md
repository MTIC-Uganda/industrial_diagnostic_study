## 6.0 Why Petrochemicals & Fertilizers Matters for the Tenfold Growth Strategy

This chain feeds two tenfold pillars at once: **Minerals/energy** (the oil refinery and petrochemical park) and **agro-industrialisation** (fertilizer for farm productivity). A ten-times-larger economy cannot run on imported fuels, imported plastics feedstock, and near-zero domestic fertilizer while farm yields stay low for want of nutrients. Uganda has the assets in motion — a **60,000 bpd refinery and the Kabalega Petrochemical Park** (investment agreement signed 2025) and **phosphate resources at Sukulu** — but petrochemicals are pre-production and fertilizer output has stalled. Sequencing these correctly (fertilizer now; petrochemicals as the refinery comes on ~2029–30) is the tenfold play.

## 6.A Value Chain Map

| Strand | Phase | Inputs | Output | Uganda's position |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Petrochemicals | Refining | Crude oil | Fuels, LPG, naphtha, base chemicals | **Pre-production** (~2029–30) |
| Petrochemicals | Petrochemical processing | Naphtha/gas | Polymers, solvents, intermediates | **Future** (Kabalega park) |
| Fertilizers | Phosphate mining & processing | Phosphate rock, sulphur | Phosphate fertilizer, sulphuric acid | **Stalled** (Sukulu) |
| Fertilizers | Blending | Imported/local nutrients | NPK blends | **Limited** |
| Both | Market | Products | Fuel, plastics feedstock, fertilizer | **Import-dependent** |

**Where value is captured and lost.** Uganda imports refined fuels, plastics feedstock (Chapter 8 linkage), and most fertilizer. Mineral/chemical fertilizer (HS 3105) imports were ~**USD 32.1 million in 2024** (ITC TradeMap), and total fertilizer need vastly exceeds domestic supply. The petrochemical base awaits the Kabaale refinery.

## 6.B Current Status

### Status snapshot

| Indicator | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Refinery | **Kabaale (Hoima), 60,000 bpd**; investment agreement Mar 2025 | UNOC; PAU |
| Refinery investor / structure | **Alpha MBM Investments (UAE)**, Govt ~40% via UNOC; ~USD 4bn; ops ~2029–30 | UNOC; PAU |
| Phosphate plant | **Sukulu/Osukuru (Tororo)**, ~100,000 t/yr design — **shut 2021, brief 2023, halted** | Osukuru records |
| Fertilizer use vs need | **~50,000 t imported vs ~1,000,000 t need** | sector; MAAIF |
| Fertilizer imports (HS 3105) | ~USD 32.1m (2024) | ITC TradeMap |

**Petrochemicals (pre-production).** Anchored on the **Kabaale refinery (60,000 bpd)** and **Kabalega Petrochemical Industrial Park**; **March 2025 implementation agreement with Alpha MBM Investments (UAE)** (Government ~40% via UNOC), ~USD 4bn, operations ~**2029–30** (UNOC; PAU). Until then, all polymers and refined products are imported.

**Fertilizers (stalled).** The **Sukulu/Osukuru complex** (Guangzhou Dongsong), commissioned 2018 with ~100,000 t/yr phosphate-fertilizer design (plus sulphuric-acid and steel units), **shut in 2021 after losing financing, briefly resumed in 2023, then halted**. Uganda imports ~**50,000 t** of fertilizer against an estimated need approaching **1 million tonnes** for effective agricultural intensification.

## 6.C Market Analysis (four levels) and 2040 Projection

| Level | Size / Uganda position | Drivers |
|---|---|---|
| **Uganda (domestic)** | Huge unmet fertilizer demand (~50,000 t vs ~1m t need); fuel & plastics-feedstock demand large but awaiting refinery | Agricultural intensification; industrialisation; motorisation |
| **Regional (EAC + COMESA)** | A functioning refinery would supply regional fuel/petrochemicals; fertilizer blending could serve regional agriculture (post-2029) | Regional fuel deficit; agriculture |
| **Continental (Africa)** | Africa is a large fertilizer importer; AfCFTA access | Food-security drive; fertilizer localisation |
| **Global** | Not relevant medium-term; chain is about domestic/regional substitution | — |

### 2040 projection — Tenfold logic

Agricultural intensification toward tenfold farm output implies fertilizer demand rising from today's ~1m t *need* toward **~1.5–2 million tonnes a year by 2040**, while a refinery + petrochemical park would localise a large fuels-and-polymers market (feedstock for the Plastics chain, Chapter 8). The sequence is decisive: **fertilizer first** (food security can't wait for 2029), **petrochemicals as the refinery comes online**. Other markets grow on business-as-usual.

## 6.D Binding Constraints — Weighted

| # | Constraint (category) | Weight | Evidence / gravity |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Stalled phosphate processing / fertilizer supply (inputs)** | **5** | Sukulu shut since 2021/23; ~50,000 t vs ~1m t need — a food-security and productivity constraint |
| 2 | **Very large capital + financing fragility (finance)** | **5** | Refinery ~USD 4bn; Sukulu's collapse shows financing fragility; both strands capital-intensive |
| 3 | **Feedstock timing — refinery pre-2029 (inputs/sequencing)** | **4** | No domestic petrochemical feedstock until ~2029–30; premature downstream investment is risky |
| 4 | **Fertilizer quality & distribution (standards/logistics)** | **4** | Adulterated fertilizer a market problem (UNBS); rural distribution to farmers weak |
| 5 | **Environmental management (waste)** | **3** | Refinery emissions; phosphogypsum from phosphate processing; NEMA-regulated |
| 6 | **Skills (skills)** | **3** | Chemical/process-engineering scarce; oil sector building some capacity |
| 7 | **Energy/process power (energy)** | **2** | Refinery self-supplies; fertilizer processing needs reliable power |

The near-term binding constraint is **fertilizer supply** (resolve Sukulu or enable alternative blending); the medium-term one is **sequencing petrochemicals to the refinery** without over-committing capital early.

## 6.E Greening & Sustainability

This is the most carbon-exposed chain in the report — which makes greening both a licence-to-operate and a competitiveness play (CBAM increasingly prices carbon in fertilizer/fuel exports).

| Dimension | Status / opportunity |
|---|---|
| **Current status** | Pre-production refinery; stalled phosphate plant; high-emission processes by nature |
| **Current solutions** | NEMA environmental governance; gas-capture design intent at the refinery |
| **Emerging solutions (competitiveness enablers)** | (1) **Green ammonia/nitrogen fertilizer** via hydro-powered electrolysis — a genuine low-carbon fertilizer route for Uganda's grid; (2) **gas capture (not flaring)** for LPG and fertilizer feedstock; (3) **phosphogypsum reuse** into gypsum board/cement (links to Cement, Chapter 9); (4) **refinery emissions control and water reuse** |

**Why it pays:** green ammonia turns Uganda's clean hydro power into low-carbon fertilizer (import-substitution *and* an exportable green product), and phosphogypsum reuse converts a hazardous by-product into building-materials feedstock.

## 6.F Prioritized Products — through the Tenfold Lens

| Product / sub-sector | Market | Comp. adv. | Feasibility | Jobs | Import subst. | Green fit | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Fertilizer (blending + phosphate revival)** | Very high (unmet) | Medium (phosphate rock) | Medium | Medium | High | Medium–High (green ammonia) | **Priority** |
| **Sulphuric acid & industrial chemicals** | Medium | Medium | Medium | Low | Medium | Medium | **Priority (paired w/ fertilizer)** |
| **Petrochemicals / polymers (Kabalega park)** | High (post-2029) | High (future feedstock) | Low now | High | Very high | Medium | **Priority (medium-term, sequenced)** |
| Large-scale refined-product export | Medium | Low now | Low | Medium | Medium | Low | Conditional (post-refinery) |
| Greenfield specialty chemicals | Low | Low | Low | Low | Low | — | Deprioritize |

**The three priorities for Petrochemicals & Fertilizers:**

1. **Fertilizer — blending plus revival/replacement of phosphate processing** — highest-urgency, highest-impact near-term opportunity, tied to agricultural productivity; resolve the Sukulu stall or enable alternative blending (including a green-ammonia route).
2. **Sulphuric acid and basic industrial chemicals** — paired with phosphate processing; useful across industry.
3. **Petrochemicals/polymers at Kabalega** — the transformational medium-term prize, sequenced to the refinery (~2029–30); prepare the park, skills and downstream plastics linkage now (Chapter 8).

**Explicit deprioritization.** Uganda should **not** pursue greenfield specialty chemicals or large-scale refined-product export ahead of the refinery. Near-term focus is **fertilizer for domestic agriculture**; petrochemicals must be **sequenced**, not rushed.

## 6.G Project Profiles

**P1 — Fertilizer supply: phosphate revival + blending (strategic anchor + green).**
- *Strategic:* attacks the ~50,000 t vs ~1m t gap; directly raises farm productivity (agro-industrialisation).
- *Quick-win element:* expand blending and quality enforcement now; restructure/re-tender Sukulu.
- *Green:* green-ammonia (hydro electrolysis) nitrogen route; phosphogypsum reuse.

**P2 — Kabalega petrochemical park readiness (strategic, sequenced).**
- *Strategic:* localises fuels and plastics feedstock once the refinery runs; anchors the Plastics chain.
- *Quick-win element:* park infrastructure, skills and downstream linkages prepared pre-2029.
- *Green:* gas capture; emissions control designed in.

**P3 — Green-ammonia pilot (green + strategic).**
- *Strategic:* low-carbon nitrogen fertilizer from hydro power; import substitution + export potential.
- *Quick-win element:* modular electrolysis pilot.
- *Green:* near-zero-carbon fertilizer.
