## 6.A Value Chain Map

This chain has two related but distinct strands: **petrochemicals** (derived from oil and gas) and **fertilizers** (mineral- and chemical-based agricultural inputs). Uganda is **pre-production in petrochemicals** (refinery under development) and has **stalled domestic fertilizer production**, importing most chemical fertilizer.

| Strand | Phase | Inputs | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Petrochemicals | Refining | Crude oil | Fuels, Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG), naphtha, base chemicals |
| Petrochemicals | Petrochemical processing | Naphtha/gas | Polymers, solvents, intermediates |
| Fertilizers | Phosphate mining & processing | Phosphate rock, sulphur | Phosphate fertilizer, sulphuric acid |
| Fertilizers | Blending | Imported/local nutrients | Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium (NPK) blends |
| Both | Market | Products | Fuel, plastics feedstock, fertilizer |

**Where value is captured and lost.** Uganda currently imports refined fuels, plastics feedstock (Chapter 8 linkage), and most fertilizer. Phosphatic/other mineral fertilizer imports (HS 3105) were about **USD 32.1 million in 2024** (ITC TradeMap), and total fertilizer need vastly exceeds domestic supply. The future petrochemical base depends on the Kabaale refinery reaching production.

---

## 6.B Current State Assessment

**Petrochemicals (pre-production).** Uganda's petrochemical future is anchored on the **Kabaale (Hoima) refinery and the Kabalega Petrochemical Industrial Park**. The refinery is designed for **60,000 barrels per day**; in **March 2025 an implementation agreement was signed with the UAE's Alpha MBM Investments** (with Government holding ~40% via UNOC), at an estimated USD 4 billion, with operations targeted for around **2029–2030** (UNOC; PAU; Hydrocarbon Engineering, 2025). Until then, Uganda has no domestic refining or petrochemical feedstock; all polymers and refined products are imported.

**Fertilizers (stalled domestic production).** The **Sukulu / Osukuru Industrial Complex** in Tororo (operated by Guangzhou Dongsong) was commissioned in 2018 with a design phosphate-fertilizer capacity of ~100,000 t/year (and associated sulphuric-acid and steel units). However, the plant **shut in 2021 after losing financing, resumed only briefly in 2023, and then halted again** — so domestic fertilizer output is far below both design capacity and national need (Osukuru complex records; Milling Middle East & Africa, 2023). Uganda imports about 50,000 t of fertilizer against an estimated need approaching 1 million tonnes for effective agricultural intensification.

**Trade position (ITC TradeMap, 2024).** Mineral/chemical fertilizer (HS 3105) imports ~USD 32.1m; broader fertilizer and refined-product imports add to this. Uganda is a **net importer across the chain**.

## 6.C Binding Constraints Analysis

| Dimension | Assessment |
|---|---|
| **Inputs / raw materials** | Petrochemical feedstock awaits the refinery (≥2029); phosphate rock exists at Sukulu but the plant is stalled; nutrient inputs for blending are imported. |
| **Technology & productivity** | Refinery/petrochemical technology is world-class-capital-intensive and investor-supplied; Sukulu's processing has under-performed. |
| **Energy** | Both strands are energy-intensive; the refinery will be self-supplying; fertilizer processing needs reliable power. |
| **Water** | Process water needed; managed under NEMA. |
| **Logistics** | Refinery linked to the crude pipeline and product distribution; fertilizer distribution to farmers is a rural-logistics challenge. |
| **Waste & effluent** | Significant environmental management (refinery emissions, phosphogypsum from phosphate processing); NEMA-regulated. |
| **Finance & investment** | Very large capital needs; refinery financed via the Alpha MBM agreement; Sukulu's collapse shows financing fragility. |
| **Skills & workforce** | Chemical/process-engineering skills scarce; the oil sector is building some capacity. |
| **Standards & quality** | Fertilizer quality standards (UNBS) and fuel specifications critical; sub-standard/adulterated fertilizer is a market problem. |
| **Policy & regulatory** | Oil-sector governance (PAU, UNOC), fertilizer subsidy/quality policy, and agricultural-input programmes are decisive. |

## 6.D Market Assessment

**Domestic.** Large unmet fertilizer demand (need approaching 1m t vs ~50,000 t imported) is the clearest immediate market — an agricultural-productivity imperative. Refined-product and plastics-feedstock demand is also large but awaits the refinery.

**Regional (EAC/COMESA).** A functioning refinery would supply regional fuel and petrochemical markets; fertilizer blending could serve regional agriculture. These are post-2029 prospects for petrochemicals.

**Global.** Not relevant in the medium term; the chain is about domestic/regional substitution.

**Accessible market conclusion.** In the near term, prioritize **fertilizer supply for domestic agriculture** (revive/replace Sukulu; expand blending). In the medium term, the **Kabalega petrochemical park** becomes the anchor once the refinery is operational.

## 6.E Prioritized Products

| Product / sub-sector | Market | Comp. adv. | Feasibility | Jobs | Import subst. | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Fertilizer (blending + phosphate revival)** | Very high (unmet demand) | Medium (phosphate rock) | Medium | Medium | High | **Priority** |
| **Sulphuric acid & industrial chemicals** | Medium | Medium | Medium | Low | Medium | **Priority (paired with fertilizer)** |
| **Petrochemicals / polymers (Kabalega park)** | High (post-2029) | High (future feedstock) | Low now | High | Very high | **Priority (medium-term, sequenced)** |
| Large-scale refined-product export | Medium | Low now | Low | Medium | Medium | Conditional (post-refinery) |
| Greenfield API/specialty chemicals | Low | Low | Low | Low | Low | Deprioritize |

**The three priorities for Petrochemicals & Fertilizers:**

1. **Fertilizer — blending plus revival/replacement of phosphate processing** — the highest-urgency, highest-impact near-term opportunity, tied directly to agricultural productivity and the agro-industrialization agenda. Resolve the Sukulu stall or enable alternative blending capacity.
2. **Sulphuric acid and basic industrial chemicals** — naturally paired with phosphate processing and useful across industry.
3. **Petrochemicals/polymers at the Kabalega park** — the transformational medium-term prize, sequenced to the refinery (~2029–30); preparing the park, skills, and downstream plastics linkage now (Chapter 8).

**Explicit deprioritization.** Uganda should **not** pursue greenfield specialty-chemical or large-scale refined-product export ambitions ahead of the refinery. Near-term focus belongs on **fertilizer for domestic agriculture**; petrochemicals are real but must be **sequenced** to the refinery timeline rather than pursued prematurely.
