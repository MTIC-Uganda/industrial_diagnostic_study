# Annex A: Value Chain Status Reports (Sector Snapshots)

These one-page **sector snapshots** distil the current status, market, constraints, priorities and green opportunity for each value chain in this report. Each is designed to be **extracted and shared independently** as a quick-reference brief. Figures and sources are detailed in the corresponding chapter (4–9) and the consolidated References.

---

## A.1 Textiles & Garments — Status Snapshot

| Indicator | Value |
|---|---|
| Cotton output | ~116,000 bales (2023); up to ~250,000 potential |
| Ginneries | **39, ~1m bales/yr capacity — at ~10%** |
| Integrated mills | 2 (Nytil; Fine Spinners) — ~10% of lint; ~USD 50m turnover |
| Lint exports (raw) | ~USD 11.3m (HS 5201); ~90% exported raw |
| Employment (Fine Spinners + outgrowers) | ~18,000 |

- **Position:** strong at raw end (growing, ginning); weak midstream (spinning, **wet-processing gap**); limited garments.
- **Top constraints (weighted):** wet-processing/finishing gap (5); used-clothing competition (4); cotton output volatility (4); capital (4).
- **Priorities:** ① Garments & apparel ② Yarn & fabric (+finishing) ③ Cottonseed oil & cake.
- **2040 (tenfold):** multi-fold apparel demand; process domestic lint → major employer.
- **Green edge:** organic/traceable cotton; closed-loop wet-processing; solar mills.

## A.2 Pharmaceuticals — Status Snapshot

| Indicator | Value |
|---|---|
| Domestic market | ~USD 221m (2023), +9.4%/yr |
| Import dependence | **~90% of medicines** (was 96% in 2016) |
| Medicaments imports (HS 3004) | **USD 263.4m** (2024) |
| WHO-GMP manufacturers | 6 (flagship: Cipla QCIL ~1.2bn tablets/yr) |

- **Position:** established formulation/packaging on imported APIs; rare regional strength (QCIL, globally prequalified).
- **Top constraints (weighted):** API import dependence (5); WHO-GMP depth (5); capital + regulatory lead time (4); procurement access (4).
- **Priorities:** ① Essential generics (ARVs, antimalarials, anti-TB, NCD) ② Medical consumables ③ Selective local API.
- **2040 (tenfold):** market ~4× to **~USD 1bn**; shift from ~90% imported to substantial local share.
- **Green edge:** solar/efficient GMP utilities; pharma-water recycling; green chemistry.

## A.3 Petrochemicals & Fertilizers — Status Snapshot

| Indicator | Value |
|---|---|
| Refinery | Kabaale 60,000 bpd; **Alpha MBM agreement Mar 2025** (~USD 4bn; ops ~2029–30) |
| Phosphate plant | Sukulu ~100,000 t/yr — **shut 2021, halted again** |
| Fertilizer use vs need | **~50,000 t vs ~1,000,000 t** |
| Fertilizer imports (HS 3105) | ~USD 32.1m (2024) |

- **Position:** pre-production petrochemicals; stalled domestic fertilizer; import-dependent.
- **Top constraints (weighted):** stalled phosphate/fertilizer supply (5); capital & financing fragility (5); feedstock timing pre-2029 (4); quality & distribution (4).
- **Priorities:** ① Fertilizer (blending + phosphate revival) ② Sulphuric acid/industrial chemicals ③ Petrochemicals/polymers (sequenced to refinery).
- **2040 (tenfold):** fertilizer demand → **~1.5–2 Mt/yr**; localised fuels/polymers post-refinery.
- **Green edge:** green-ammonia fertilizer (hydro electrolysis); gas capture; phosphogypsum reuse.

## A.4 Sugar & Confectionery — Status Snapshot

| Indicator | Value |
|---|---|
| National output | ~822,000 t (2022) |
| Consumption / surplus | ~370,000 t / **180,000+ t surplus** |
| Industrial-sugar imports (HS 1701) | ~USD 38.1m |
| Largest miller | Kakira (~half; >2m t cane/yr); Kinyara, SCOUL, Mayuge |

- **Position:** mature, competitive milling **in surplus**; under-exploited downstream.
- **Top constraints (weighted):** over-supply / over-licensing of raw sugar (5); downstream capital/technology (4); industrial-sugar standards (3); co-gen feed-in (3).
- **Priorities:** ① Industrial/refined sugar ② Ethanol ③ Bagasse co-generation.
- **2040 (tenfold):** value shifts from raw sugar to industrial sugar, ethanol, power, confectionery.
- **Green edge:** ethanol biofuel; bagasse renewable power; vinasse-to-biogas (greenest chain).

## A.5 Plastics & Packaging — Status Snapshot

| Indicator | Value |
|---|---|
| Plastics imported | ~150,000 t/yr (all virgin resin imported) |
| Packaging imports (HS 3923) | ~USD 37.1m (2024) |
| Certified bag makers | 41 firms / 67 UNBS permits |
| Plastic waste | **~600 t/day, <40% collected** |
| Policy | Circularity Strategy 2023–28; **2025 single-use-bag ban move** |

- **Position:** established conversion; 100% imported resin; under-scaled recycling; enabling industry for all chains.
- **Top constraints (weighted):** imported virgin resin (5); weak circularity/collection (4); capital & fragmentation (3); policy-transition risk (3).
- **Priorities:** ① Industrial/food-beverage packaging ② Plastics recycling ③ Agri & cement packaging.
- **2040 (tenfold):** packaging scales with all manufacturing — meet via conversion + recycled content + (later) domestic resin.
- **Green edge:** recycling/EPR (the circular flagship); recycled-content & compostable alternatives.

## A.6 Cement & Building Materials — Status Snapshot

| Indicator | Value |
|---|---|
| Market | ~USD 707m (2024), +~12% YoY |
| Clinker dependence | **>50% imported** |
| Cement & clinker (HS 2523) | imports USD 162.2m; exports USD 86.0m |
| Producers | Tororo ~3.0 Mtpa; Hima ~2.0; Simba ~1.0; Kampala ~1.0 |
| New clinker | NE plant (USD 300m); Buikwe (USD 200m, 2025) |

- **Position:** strong grinding & regional finished-cement exporter; **critical clinker gap** (highest-value step imported).
- **Top constraints (weighted):** clinker capacity gap (5); energy cost & reliability (5); capital intensity (4); limestone licensing (3).
- **Priorities:** ① Clinker production (close the gap) ② Finished cement (defend & grow export) ③ Higher-value building materials.
- **2040 (tenfold):** construction supercycle → market several-fold to **multi-billion USD/yr**.
- **Green edge:** alternative-fuel kilns; clinker substitution with steel slag (Ch.4) & phosphogypsum (Ch.6).
