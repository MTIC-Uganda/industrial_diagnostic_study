## 8.A Value Chain Map

The plastics chain runs from polymer resin (feedstock) through conversion (moulding, extrusion, film) to finished plastic goods and packaging, with a parallel recycling loop. Uganda participates at the **conversion and packaging** stage but **imports all virgin resin** and a large share of finished plastic products.

| Phase | Activity | Main inputs | Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | Resin / feedstock | Petrochemicals (imported) | Polyethylene (PE), Polypropylene (PP), Polyethylene Terephthalate (PET), Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC) resin |
| II | Conversion | Resin | Films, sheets, preforms, mouldings |
| III | Packaging & products | Converted plastic | Bottles, sacks, containers, household goods |
| IV | Use | Products | Consumer/industrial use |
| V | Recycling | Post-consumer plastic | Recycled flake/pellet |

**Where value is captured and lost.** Uganda imports **all virgin polymer resin** (no domestic production until the petrochemical park, Chapter 6) and imports a large share of finished plastics and packaging. Plastic packaging (HS 3923) imports were about **USD 37.1 million in 2024** (ITC TradeMap), and Uganda imports roughly **150,000 tonnes of plastics a year** (NEMA). Domestic value addition is in conversion; the feedstock and much finished product are imported.

## 8.B Current State Assessment

**Industry.** Uganda has an established plastics-conversion industry concentrated in Kampala, Wakiso, Jinja, and Mukono. **UNBS reports 41 certified manufacturers of plastic carrier and flat bags holding 67 valid permits**, indicating a substantial formal sector (UNBS, via sector reporting). Leading firms include **Nice House of Plastics** (the largest, established 1970), **Nile Plastic Industries**, and packaging converters serving the beverage, food, and agricultural-input sectors. Recyclers such as **Quality Plastics (Mukono)** operate a growing but under-scaled recycling loop.

**Waste and circularity.** Uganda generates about **600 tonnes of plastic waste per day, of which under 40% is properly collected and managed** (NEMA). The **National Strategy for Plastics Circularity (2023–2028)** sets the policy direction; if fully implemented, it could cut plastic pollution by up to ~37% by 2040 (NEMA).

**2025 policy shift.** In 2025, **NEMA moved to push a ban on single-use plastic carrier bags** (amending the National Environment Act), aligning Uganda with EAC neighbours (Kenya, Rwanda, Tanzania). This reshapes the sector: constraining single-use bags while creating demand for **alternatives, recycled content, and durable packaging** (NEMA; Margherita News, 2025).

**Trade position (ITC TradeMap, 2024).** Plastic packaging (HS 3923) imports ~USD 37.1m; broader resin and finished-plastic imports are large; exports are modest and regional. Uganda is a **net importer at both the feedstock and finished-product ends**.

## 8.C Binding Constraints Analysis

| Dimension | Assessment |
|---|---|
| **Inputs / raw materials** | 100% imported virgin resin — the core constraint; domestic resin awaits the petrochemical park (Chapter 6). Recycled feedstock is under-developed. |
| **Technology & productivity** | Conversion technology is established; recycling and high-spec packaging technology need upgrading. |
| **Energy** | Conversion is moderately energy-intensive; reliability matters. |
| **Water** | Not a binding constraint. |
| **Logistics** | Imported resin bears the landlocked penalty, raising input costs. |
| **Waste & effluent** | The defining issue — low collection rates, pollution; also the recycling opportunity. NEMA-regulated. |
| **Finance & investment** | Recycling plants and modern converters need capital; sector is fragmented. |
| **Skills & workforce** | Polymer-processing and recycling skills limited. |
| **Standards & quality** | UNBS certification (bag thickness, food-grade) is central; sub-standard bags are both a quality and environmental problem. |
| **Policy & regulatory** | The 2025 single-use-ban trajectory and circularity strategy are the dominant policy forces — risk for single-use producers, opportunity for recyclers and alternatives. |

## 8.D Market Assessment

**Domestic.** Large and growing demand for packaging (beverage, food, agricultural inputs, cement bags, pharmaceuticals) and household plastics — much met by imports. Recycled-content and durable packaging demand will rise with the policy shift.

**Regional (EAC/COMESA).** Regional packaging demand is accessible; alignment with EAC plastic-bag bans creates a level playing field for compliant alternatives.

**Global.** Not relevant; the chain is domestic/regional substitution and circularity.

**Accessible market conclusion.** Prioritize **value-added and industrial packaging** (food, beverage, agri-inputs, pharma), **plastics recycling** (turning the waste problem into feedstock), and **alternatives to single-use bags** aligned with the 2025 policy.

## 8.E Prioritized Products

| Product / sub-sector | Market | Comp. adv. | Feasibility | Jobs | Import subst. | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Industrial & food/beverage packaging** | High (USD 37m+) | Medium | High | High | High | **Priority** |
| **Plastics recycling (flake/pellet)** | High (circularity) | Medium | Medium | High | High (feedstock) | **Priority** |
| **Agricultural & cement packaging (sacks, films)** | High | Medium | High | High | High | **Priority** |
| Single-use carrier bags | Declining (ban) | — | — | — | — | Deprioritize |
| Domestic virgin-resin production | High | Low now | Low | Medium | Very high | Conditional (sequence to refinery, Ch.6) |

**The three priorities for Plastics & Packaging:**

1. **Industrial and food/beverage packaging** — substitute the USD 37m+ packaging import line with higher-value, standards-compliant domestic packaging for Uganda's food, beverage, and agri-input sectors.
2. **Plastics recycling** — scale recycling to turn the 600 t/day waste stream into domestic feedstock, directly supporting circularity and reducing resin imports; the strongest alignment with the 2025 policy.
3. **Agricultural and cement packaging** (woven sacks, films, mulch) — feasible, job-rich, and linked to Uganda's agriculture and cement sectors.

**Explicit deprioritization.** Uganda should **not** invest in expanding **single-use carrier-bag** capacity — the 2025 policy trajectory makes it a stranded-asset risk. Domestic **virgin-resin** production is a conditional priority that must be **sequenced to the petrochemical park** (Chapter 6), not pursued independently.

## 8.F Priority Action Matrix

| Timeframe | Action | Lead | Indicative cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Quick win (0–12m)** | Clear, phased single-use-bag rules with support for transition to alternatives/recycled content | NEMA, MTIC | Low (policy) |
| Quick win (0–12m) | Strengthen UNBS enforcement on packaging standards (food-grade, thickness) | UNBS | Low |
