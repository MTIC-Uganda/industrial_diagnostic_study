## 11.1 Purpose

This chapter consolidates the prioritized products from Chapters 4–9 into an investment portfolio and phased roadmap for the six Report 2 chains. Project profiles cover the **priority products only**.

## 11.3 Project Profiles (Priority Products Only)

### Profile 1 — Spinning/Weaving + Garment Capacity (Textiles)
- **Business case:** ~90% of cotton lint is exported raw (~USD 11.3m, 2024); domestic processing captures the value gap to yarn, fabric, and garments. Institutional uniform and regional apparel demand anchor sales.
- **Anchors:** Southern Range Nyanza (Nytil), Fine Spinners, Lira spinning facility.
- **Modality:** Private-led + PPP (wet-processing/effluent infrastructure). **Indicative CAPEX:** USD 100–250m. **Key risk:** cotton supply volatility, wet-processing/effluent. **Mitigation:** agronomy programme, shared effluent treatment.

### Profile 2 — GMP Essential-Generics Expansion (Pharmaceuticals)
- **Business case:** ~90% of medicines imported; medicaments imports USD 263.4m (2024). Expanding WHO-GMP generics (ARVs, antimalarials, anti-TB, NCD) substitutes imports and serves donor/regional markets.
- **Anchor:** Cipla QCIL (~1.2bn tablets/yr) and other GMP firms.
- **Modality:** Private-led + PPP (GMP-upgrade fund). **Indicative CAPEX:** USD 50–150m. **Key risk:** API dependence, regulatory lead time. **Mitigation:** preferential procurement, prequalification support.

### Profile 3 — Fertilizer Revival/Blending (Petrochem/Fertilizers)
- **Business case:** Demand approaching 1m t vs ~50,000 t imported; phosphate fertilizer imports (HS 3105) USD 32.1m (2024). Reviving/replacing Sukulu and expanding blending serves agricultural productivity.
- **Modality:** PPP / private-led. **Indicative CAPEX:** USD 100–300m. **Key risk:** the Sukulu financing collapse precedent. **Mitigation:** restructure or re-tender; start with blending capacity.

### Profile 4 — Refinery & Petrochemical Park (Petrochem/Fertilizers)
- **Business case:** 60,000 bpd refinery + Kabalega Petrochemical Industrial Park; domestic fuels, polymers (plastics feedstock), and chemical inputs.
- **Anchor:** Alpha MBM implementation agreement, March 2025 (~USD 4bn; Government ~40% via UNOC); operations ~2029–30.
- **Modality:** Investor + UNOC (PPP). **Sequencing note:** the medium-term enabler for Profiles 3 and 6.

### Profile 5 — Industrial Sugar + Ethanol + Co-generation (Sugar)
- **Business case:** Structural surplus (~822k t output vs ~370k t consumption) plus USD 38.1m industrial-sugar imports; downstream products absorb the surplus into higher value.
- **Anchors:** Kakira, Kinyara, SCOUL, Mayuge.
- **Modality:** Private-led. **Indicative CAPEX:** USD 50–150m. **High-certainty** given existing cane and mills.

### Profile 6 — Plastics Recycling + Value-Added Packaging (Plastics)
- **Business case:** ~150,000 t/yr plastics imported; packaging imports (HS 3923) USD 37.1m (2024); 600 t/day waste, <40% collected. Recycling substitutes resin imports; value-added packaging substitutes finished imports; both align with the 2025 circularity policy.
- **Modality:** Private-led (EPR framework). **Indicative CAPEX:** USD 30–80m. **High alignment** with policy direction.

### Profile 7 — Clinker Capacity Build-out (Cement)
- **Business case:** >50% of clinker imported; cement/clinker imports (HS 2523) USD 162.2m (2024) against USD 86.0m exports. Domestic clinker closes the gap and secures feedstock for grinders.
- **Anchors:** USD 300m NE plant; West International USD 200m Buikwe plant.
- **Modality:** Private-led. **Indicative CAPEX:** USD 200–300m+. **Key risk:** energy cost. **Mitigation:** energy agreements, alternative fuels.

### Profile 8 — Higher-Value Building Materials (Cement)
- **Business case:** Fibre-cement, tiles, and precast add value and substitute construction-material imports.
- **Modality:** Private-led. **Indicative CAPEX:** USD 20–60m.

## 11.4 Implementation Roadmap

| Horizon | Agro-processing (Textiles, Sugar) | Health & Inputs (Pharma, Fertilizer) | Construction-linked (Cement, Plastics) | Petrochemicals |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Year 1 (quick wins)** | Uniform procurement; cotton agronomy; sugar licensing discipline + ethanol mandate | NMS/JMS local-medicine procurement; resolve Sukulu; fertilizer quality enforcement | UNBS enforcement; support new clinker plants; phased single-use-bag rules | Park readiness planning |
| **Years 2–3 (reforms + first capital)** | Wet-processing/effluent infra; textile & food-tech skills | GMP-upgrade fund; fertilizer strategy/subsidy | EPR & recycling framework; energy agreements for clinker | Downstream linkage prep |
| **Years 4–5 (major investment)** | Spinning/weaving + garment park; industrial sugar/ethanol/co-gen | GMP generics expansion; phosphate/blending capacity | Clinker build-out; recycling & packaging; building materials | Refinery + petrochemical park commissioning |

**Sequencing principle.** Quick wins are policy and procurement (low cost, high signal). Years 2–3 build standards, skills, finance, and infrastructure. Years 4–5 are the major capital builds — with the refinery/petrochemical park as the medium-term feedstock anchor that should pull plastics and fertilizer investment into its orbit.

---

## 11.5 Indicative Portfolio Total

Excluding the investor-financed refinery (~USD 4bn, separately anchored), the seven other priority investments represent an **indicative total in the order of USD 0.6–1.3 billion** over five years, the majority **private-led**, with public/PPP support concentrated in **standards, skills, effluent/circular-economy infrastructure, blended finance, and guaranteed procurement**. All figures are indicative and require feasibility confirmation.
