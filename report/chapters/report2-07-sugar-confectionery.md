## 7.0 Why Sugar & Confectionery Matters for the Tenfold Growth Strategy

Sugar is Uganda's clearest agro-industrial success — a **mature, competitive milling industry already in structural surplus**. That changes the tenfold question for this chain: not "grow more cane," but **"convert the surplus into higher-value, greener products"** — industrial sugar, ethanol, grid electricity from bagasse, and confectionery. Done well, sugar becomes a model of the Tenfold Strategy's logic: take what Uganda already produces in abundance and move it up the value chain into energy, chemicals and food products, rather than exporting a low-value commodity.

## 7.A Value Chain Map

| Phase | Activity | Main inputs | Output | Uganda's position |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| I | Cane growing | Land, inputs | Sugarcane | **Strong** (surplus) |
| II | Milling | Cane | Raw/brown sugar, molasses, bagasse | **Mature/competitive** |
| III | Refining | Raw sugar | Refined/industrial sugar | **Gap** — imported |
| IV | Downstream | Sugar, molasses, bagasse | Confectionery, ethanol, electricity (co-gen) | **Under-exploited** |
| V | Market | Products | Domestic + regional | **Active**; surplus exporter |

**Where value is captured and lost.** Uganda produces a surplus of brown sugar yet still imports **industrial/refined sugar** and confectionery and under-exploits downstream products (ethanol, co-generation). Sugar imports (HS 1701) were ~**USD 38.1 million in 2024** — largely industrial sugar — even as Uganda exports surplus brown sugar regionally (ITC TradeMap). The leak here is *under-diversification*, not under-production.

## 7.B Current Status

### Status snapshot

| Indicator | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| National output | **~822,000 t (2022)** (~720k brown + ~102k industrial) | USMA; sector |
| Domestic consumption | ~370,000 t | sector |
| Structural surplus | **180,000+ t** | sector |
| Industrial-sugar imports (HS 1701) | ~USD 38.1m (2024) | ITC TradeMap |
| Largest miller | **Kakira Sugar Works** (~half of output; >2m t cane/yr) | USMA; Kakira |
| Other majors | Kinyara (industrial sugar ~75,000 t, 2023), SCOUL, Mayuge, smaller mills | USMA |

**Production and surplus.** National output ~**822,000 t (2022)** against consumption ~**370,000 t**, leaving a structural **surplus of 180,000+ t**; continued licensing of new mills has created over-supply and price pressure — the live policy issue.

**Firms.** **Kakira Sugar Works** (~half of output; crushing >2m t cane/yr), **Kinyara Sugar** (industrial-sugar refinery expanded to ~75,000 t in 2023), **Sugar Corporation of Uganda (SCOUL)**, **Mayuge Sugar**, plus smaller mills.

**Downstream.** Molasses supports ethanol; bagasse supports co-generation (grid power); confectionery is growing but still import-supplied.

## 7.C Market Analysis (four levels) and 2040 Projection

| Level | Size / Uganda position | Drivers |
|---|---|---|
| **Uganda (domestic)** | Brown sugar over-supplied; genuine gaps in **industrial/refined sugar** (USD 38m) and confectionery; ethanol-blending market latent | Food processing, beverages, pharma, fuel blending |
| **Regional (EAC + COMESA)** | Surplus brown sugar exported to Kenya, S. Sudan, DRC (subject to EAC dynamics); ethanol/industrial-sugar demand | Regional deficits; integration |
| **Continental (Africa)** | Net sugar-importing continent; AfCFTA access for refined/industrial sugar and ethanol | Africa demand growth |
| **Global** | Distorted, scale-driven raw-sugar market — not accessible | — |

### 2040 projection — Tenfold logic

A ten-times-larger economy shifts sugar's value from raw commodity to **industrial sugar (food/beverage/pharma), ethanol (fuel blending as motorisation rises), grid power (bagasse co-gen), and confectionery**. The brown-sugar surplus becomes the feedstock for these higher-value streams; by 2040 the chain's value should be dominated by downstream products, not raw sugar. Other markets grow on business-as-usual; Uganda's external plays are **regional industrial sugar/ethanol**, not global raw sugar.

## 7.D Binding Constraints — Weighted

| # | Constraint (category) | Weight | Evidence / gravity |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Over-supply / over-licensing of raw sugar (policy)** | **5** | Surplus 180,000+ t depressing prices; continued mill licensing strains cane per mill — the central policy problem |
| 2 | **Downstream capital & technology (finance/technology)** | **4** | Refining-to-industrial-grade, ethanol distilleries, confectionery need investment; milling is well-capitalised |
| 3 | **Industrial-sugar quality/standards (standards)** | **3** | Food/pharma buyers require industrial-grade specs (UNBS); current output mostly table grade |
| 4 | **Grid feed-in for co-generation (policy/energy)** | **3** | Bagasse power needs a clear feed-in/tariff framework to monetise |
| 5 | **Distillery effluent (waste)** | **3** | Vinasse and bagasse ash need management; also a circular-economy opportunity |
| 6 | **Out-grower coordination & cane quality (inputs)** | **2** | Ample cane but variable quality and out-grower coordination |
| 7 | **Skills (skills)** | **2** | Food-technology/process skills for downstream diversification |

The decisive lever is **policy on over-supply** (discipline new raw-sugar licensing) paired with **incentives to move downstream** (ethanol mandate, co-gen feed-in, industrial-sugar standards).

## 7.E Greening & Sustainability

Sugar is potentially the **greenest chain in the report** — its by-products are renewable energy and biofuel:

| Dimension | Status / opportunity |
|---|---|
| **Current status** | Some bagasse co-generation and molasses-ethanol already exist; vinasse effluent is the main impact |
| **Current solutions** | Bagasse-to-grid co-generation (renewable power); molasses ethanol |
| **Emerging solutions (competitiveness enablers)** | (1) **Fuel-blending ethanol** to decarbonise transport and cut fuel imports; (2) **expanded bagasse co-generation** feeding renewable power to the grid; (3) **vinasse-to-biogas and bio-fertilizer** (closes the loop, links to the Fertilizer chain); (4) **circular mill design** (zero-waste) |

**Why it pays:** co-gen and ethanol turn waste streams into saleable energy and fuel, improving mill economics while delivering renewable power and lower transport emissions — green and profitable at once.

## 7.F Prioritized Products — through the Tenfold Lens

| Product / sub-sector | Market | Comp. adv. | Feasibility | Jobs | Import subst. | Green fit | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Industrial / refined sugar** | High (USD 38m imports) | High (surplus cane) | High | Medium | High | Medium | **Priority** |
| **Ethanol (fuel & industrial)** | High (blending, regional) | High (molasses) | High | Medium | High | High | **Priority** |
| **Co-generation (bagasse to grid)** | Medium–High | High (bagasse) | High | Low | Energy benefit | High | **Priority** |
| **Confectionery** | Medium | Medium | Medium | High | Medium | Medium | Conditional |
| Raw brown-sugar capacity expansion | Low (surplus) | — | — | — | — | — | Deprioritize |

**The three priorities for Sugar & Confectionery:**

1. **Industrial/refined sugar** — substitute the USD 38m import line using surplus domestic cane; serves beverages, food processing and pharmaceuticals.
2. **Ethanol** — convert molasses surplus into fuel-blending ethanol (energy security, decarbonisation) and industrial alcohol, with strong regional demand.
3. **Co-generation** — monetise bagasse as renewable grid electricity, improving mill economics.

**Explicit deprioritization.** Uganda should **not** license or expand additional raw **brown-sugar** capacity — the sector is already in surplus and over-supply is depressing prices. The strategic move is **downstream, not more raw sugar.**

## 7.G Project Profiles

**P1 — Ethanol distillery + fuel-blending programme (strategic + green).**
- *Strategic:* absorbs molasses surplus; energy security; regional ethanol demand.
- *Quick-win element:* a fuel-blending mandate creates instant guaranteed demand.
- *Green:* biofuel displacing imported petrol; vinasse-to-biogas.

**P2 — Industrial-sugar refining (strategic, import-substitution).**
- *Strategic:* substitutes the USD 38m industrial-sugar import line for food/beverage/pharma.
- *Quick-win element:* build on existing mills (Kinyara) and surplus cane.
- *Green:* co-located with co-gen for renewable process power.

**P3 — Bagasse co-generation expansion (green + quick win).**
- *Strategic:* renewable grid power + better mill economics.
- *Quick-win element:* needs mainly a clear feed-in framework.
- *Green:* renewable electricity from a waste stream.
