## C. INTEGRITY ISSUES — must fix before/at writing (these protect credibility)

These are not data gaps; they are claims in the approved documents that, if left
unaddressed, would make the report misrepresent how the study was done. For a
decision-grade government report, that matters.

### C1. Stale baseline figure in BOTH structures 🔴
Both structures (Ch2) state **"manufacturing at 16% of GDP, 30% capacity
utilization."** Latest is **15.2% (2024, World Bank)**. The 30% figure is
unverified. **Fix:** update to 15.2% and source the capacity figure from the
latest UBOS abstract, or attribute it explicitly with its year.

### C2. Methodology promises fieldwork & a validation workshop that have not happened 🔴
Both inception reports and structures state the study includes:
- "**key informant interviews**" with dozens of named firms/MDAs
- "**targeted field visits** to representative firms and clusters"
- "Findings were **validated through a structured stakeholder workshop**"

As things stand, the analysis is **desk-based** (Jerome's documents + TradeMap +
authoritative web sources). KIIs and the workshop are **planned, not done**.
**This is the single most important integrity point.** We must NOT write the
methodology in the past tense as if fieldwork occurred. Options:
- (a) Honestly describe methodology as desk-based, with KIIs/workshop as a
  validation step still to occur (recommended, truthful); or
- (b) Jerome actually convenes the KIIs/workshop before finalization; or
- (c) Clearly label the report a "draft diagnostic for validation."
**Decision needed from Solomon/Jerome.**

### C3. "Primary data" framing in inception reports 🟡
Inception reports list KIIs as primary sources (e.g. "40 steel firms"). Where
those interviews are actually drawn from Jerome's NPA/UDC report rather than
conducted by us, we should cite the **report**, not imply we interviewed the
firms. Keeps provenance honest.

### C4. Trade data source consistency 🟢 (good, just confirm)
Structures cite "UN Comtrade / ITC Trade Map." We are using **ITC TradeMap**
(same underlying data) and cite it inline. Consistent. Keep wording aligned.

## D. EXECUTION RISKS — deliverable, but need a decision

### D1. Timeline in approved docs is in the past 🔴
Both inception reports show chapters being drafted **2–8 June 2026**. It is now
later than that schedule. The workplan dates need refreshing to a realistic new
sequence before these are shown again to the TWG/Commissioner.

### D2. Two reports, 9 chains, ~19 chapters — sequencing
Achievable with the one-graph-drives-both pipeline, but should be sequenced
best-data-first: **Iron & Steel → Automotive → Sugar → Textiles → Cement →
Pharma → Plastics → Copper → Petrochemicals/Fertilizers.** (Copper and
petrochemicals last as their *current state* is thinnest — though that thinness
is itself the finding.)

### D3. Investment portfolio CAPEX/OPEX numbers
Promised per-investment CAPEX/OPEX, financing, risk. These must be sourced or
clearly labelled **indicative/illustrative** — never invented as if precise.
For a decision document, a wrong investment figure is worse than a ranged one.

### D4. Per-investment project profiles (1–2 pages each)
Potentially 20–40 profiles across both reports. Confirm scope: profiles for the
**priority products only** (≈3–4 per chain) keeps this proportionate.
