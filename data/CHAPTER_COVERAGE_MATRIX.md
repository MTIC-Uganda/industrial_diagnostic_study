# Chapter-by-Chapter Coverage Matrix

Extensive web research confirming that **every individual chapter** of both
reports has the specific information needed for its current-state, market, and
prioritization sections. This goes deeper than the earlier audits — verifying
firm names, employment, capacities, market sizes, and trade for each chapter.

Researched: 2026-06-01 (web, June 2026). Trade figures come live from ITC
TradeMap and are cited inline; the figures below are the **non-trade** specifics
that make each chapter concrete.

## REPORT 1

### Ch4 — Iron & Steel  🟢 EXCELLENT
- Installed capacity ~1.68m tpa, ~30% utilised (NPA/UDC June 2025, Jerome).
- **Firms:** Steel & Tube Industries (~1,080 staff, ~6,000 beneficiaries),
  Roofings Ltd/Roofings Rolling Mills (~1,850 staff, 2021), Tembo Steel
  (only primary producer; 2nd DRI plant Jan 2025 ~250k t), Uganda Baati,
  Yogi Steels, Abyssinia, SINO Minerals.
- **2025 investments:** Devki Group $500m integrated complex (Tororo/Mbarara,
  ~15,000 jobs projected), broke ground Nov 2025.
- Uganda imports 80–90% of steel demand (>500k t/yr).
- Sources: [UDB-STIL](https://www.udbl.co.ug/project/steel-and-tube-industries-ltd/), [African Business](https://african.business/2021/07/energy-resources/construction-boom-drives-growth-for-uganda-steel-manufacturer), [GMK Devki](https://gmk.center/en/news/a-steel-plant-will-be-built-in-uganda-with-investments-of-500-million/), [UBA manufacturing paper](https://ugandabankers.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/12/Unlocking-the-Potential-of-Uganda%E2%80%99s-Manufacturing-Sector-UBA-WP.pdf). Trade: TradeMap (72/73).

### Ch5 — Copper & Allied Metals  🟢 GOOD (was thinnest — now solid)
- **Reserves:** Kilembe 6.5m t ore @1.77% Cu + cobalt (DGSM); Mar 2025
  Sarrai/Nile Fibreboard production-sharing deal.
- **Downstream (the prioritization story):** Cable Corporation Ltd (Mehta
  Group, Uganda's first cable factory est. 1968, **~65% domestic market
  share**, supplies UMEME/UEDCL/UETCL); East African Cables (regional).
- Minimal domestic refining/cathode — value leaves unprocessed (a finding).
- Sources: [DGSM](https://dgsm.go.ug/redevelopment-of-kilembe-mines/), [Cable Corp Uganda](https://cablecorp-uganda.com/pages/about-us), [Mehta Group](https://www.mehtagroup.com/electric_cables.html). Trade: TradeMap (74).

### Ch6 — Automotive  🟢 EXCELLENT (fully refreshed from 2017)
- **Kiira Motors plant commissioned 27 Sept 2025** ("Africa's most advanced");
  installed capacity 2,500 veh/yr → 10,000 medium term; 37 EVs + 27 diesel
  buses built to date; Kayoola EV buses; UGX 50bn FY25/26 funding gap.
- **Market:** ~48,733 used vehicles imported/yr (Japan, Singapore, UK, SA);
  used-import dependency is the core issue.
- **Assembly/parts:** Auto Dealers Ltd assembles Bajaj/TVS motorcycles; active
  boda-boda spare-parts trade (Ndeeba hub).
- Sources: [Kiira (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kiira_Motors_Corporation), [UBC commissioning](https://ubc.go.ug/2025/09/27/uganda-commissions-africas-most-advanced-vehicle-plant-a-historic-milestone-for-industrialization-and-innovation/), [Mobility Foresights](https://mobilityforesights.com/product/uganda-used-car-market). Trade: TradeMap (8703/8704/8708/8711).

## REPORT 2

### Ch4 — Textiles & Garments  🟢 EXCELLENT
- **Cotton:** ~116,000 bales (2023), ~130,000 (2024 est.), up to 250,000 in
  good years; **39 ginneries, ~1m bale capacity at only ~10% utilisation**.
- **Mills:** two vertically integrated — Southern Range Nyanza (Nytil) +
  Fine Spinners (~18,000 jobs); consume only ~10% of lint, ~US$50m turnover.
- **90% of lint exported raw at <US$1/kg** — the core value-addition gap.
- Sources: [CDO/WBJ](https://worldbusinessjournal.com/cdo-leads-way/), [EPRC cotton VC](https://eprcug.org/publication/upgrading-the-cotton-value-chain-for-textile-industry-development/), [UIA-CDO](https://www.ugandainvest.go.ug/uia-cdo-team-up-to-boost-import-substitution-in-cotton-sector/). Trade: TradeMap (52/61/62).

### Ch5 — Pharmaceuticals  🟢 EXCELLENT
- **Market ~US$221m (2023)**, growing ~9.4%/yr; **import bill US$534m (2023)**.
- **~90% of medicines imported** (down from 96% in 2016) — improving but
  import-dependent.
- **Local:** 6 WHO-GMP facilities; QCIL/Cipla (~1.2bn pills/yr, ARVs/
  antimalarials/NCDs), KPI, Rene, Abacus; employment +42% (2017–19).
- Sources: [Statista](https://www.statista.com/outlook/hmo/pharmaceuticals/uganda), [UNIDO Pharma Sector Profile](https://open.unido.org/api/documents/4699932/download/Pharmaceutical%20Sector%20Profile%20-%20Uganda), [NDA register Jun 2024](https://www.nda.or.ug/). Trade: TradeMap (30).

### Ch6 — Petrochemicals & Fertilizers  🟢 GOOD (replaced 2014 data)
- **Petrochemicals:** Kabaale 60,000 bpd refinery + Kabalega Petrochemical
  Industrial Park; Alpha MBM agreement Mar 2025 ($4bn); start ~2029/30.
- **Fertilizer:** Sukulu/Osukuru (Guangzhou Dongsong) ~100k t/yr design but
  **stalled** (shut 2021, brief 2023 resumption, halted); Uganda imports ~50k t,
  needs ~1m t. Sulphuric acid co-located.
- Sources: [UNOC](https://www.unoc.co.ug/midstream/the-uganda-refinery-project/), [PAU](https://www.pau.go.ug/the-uganda-refinery-project/), [Milling MEA](https://www.millingmea.com/tororo-fertiliser-factory-in-limbo-five-months-after-resuming-operations/). Trade: TradeMap (31/2710/3901-3915).

### Ch7 — Sugar & Confectionery  🟢 EXCELLENT
- **Output ~822,000 t (2022)** (720k brown + 102k industrial); consumption
  ~370k t → structural **surplus ~180k+ t**; over-licensing of mills is the
  live policy issue.
- **Firms:** Kakira (~half of output, crushes >2m t cane/yr), Kinyara
  (industrial refinery expanded to 75k t, 2023), SCOUL, Mayuge, +many new.
- Downstream: industrial sugar, confectionery, ethanol, co-generation, bagasse.
- Sources: [Sugar industry (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sugar_industry_in_Uganda), [Monitor over-licensing](https://www.monitor.co.ug/uganda/news/national/surge-in-sugar-factories-imperils-industry-s-future-3943728), USMA 2024 (Jerome). Trade: TradeMap (1701/1704).

### Ch8 — Plastics & Packaging  🟢 GOOD
- **Imports ~150,000 t/yr** of plastics; net importer; produces 600 t plastic
  waste/day (<40% collected).
- **Firms:** Nice House of Plastics (largest, est. 1970), Nile Plastic
  Industries, Quality Plastics (recycling, Mukono); **UNBS: 41 certified
  bag manufacturers, 67 permits** (Kampala/Wakiso/Jinja/Mukono).
- **2025 policy:** NEMA pushing single-use plastic bag ban (amend NEMA Act).
- Sources: [NEMA Circularity Strategy](https://www.nema.go.ug/new_site/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/Plastic_circularity_-Report-2023-1.pdf), [Reportlinker outlook](https://www.reportlinker.com/clp/country/6347/726256), [Margherita ban](https://margheritanews.ug/2025/03/nema-pushes-for-total-polythene-bags-of-kevera-in-uganda/). Trade: TradeMap (39).

### Ch9 — Cement & Building Materials  🟢 EXCELLENT
- **Market US$707m (2024, +12% YoY)**; consumption ~2.4m+ t/yr and rising.
- **Capacity:** Tororo 3.0m, Hima ~2.0m, Simba 1.0m, Kampala Cement 1.0m +
  new $300m NE plant & West International $200m Buikwe plant (2024–25);
  **clinker import-dependent (>50%)** — the key gap.
- Limestone: Hima Dura quarry to ~2036. Broader building materials: steel
  (Ch4 link), tiles, glass, aggregates.
- Sources: [IndexBox 2025](https://www.indexbox.io/store/uganda-cement-market-analysis-forecast-size-trends-and-insights/), [CemNet country](https://www.cemnet.com/global-cement-report/country/uganda), [Wikipedia plant list](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_cement_manufacturers_in_Uganda). Trade: TradeMap (2523).
